**My Condition:**

I have secondary dystonia primarily affecting my left hand, arm, shoulder, and trapezius. The condition has been progressively worsening over several years, with severe pain from sustained involuntary contractions being the most disabling symptom. I am currently based in Naples, Florida.

I have undergone extensive treatment, including:

- **Baclofen (oral):** Tried, insufficient relief
- **Clonazepam:** This was the only medication that provided significant relief, particularly for my hand. However, I lost access when I relocated to Pittsburgh for my master's program at Carnegie Mellon University — I was unable to find a physician willing to prescribe it. This directly led to my medical withdrawal from the program.
- **Physical therapy:** Tried

I am currently managing pain with THC, which provides relief but impairs my ability to function at work. I am researching a multimodal approach to reduce dependence on any single treatment.

**Why I'm Reaching Out:**

Your research on individualized, fMRI-guided TMS for focal hand dystonia is directly relevant to my condition. I was particularly encouraged by:
- Your pilot study results showing reduced posturing, reduced pain, and improved writing legibility in focal hand dystonia patients
- The sustained benefits (2 weeks to 1 month) after a single session
- The pilot data showing low-frequency rTMS reduced muscle spasms and pain in three patients with **secondary generalized dystonia** — which closely mirrors my diagnosis
- Your accelerated protocol (NCT06015672) using four TMS sessions in a single day

**My Questions:**

1. **Am I eligible for any current or upcoming TMS trials?** My dystonia is secondary (not primary) and affects my left upper extremity. I understand your current focal hand dystonia trial may have specific inclusion criteria, but I wanted to ask directly.

2. **Does having an implanted DBS system disqualify me from TMS?** I understand there may be safety concerns about magnetic field interactions with DBS hardware. If so, are there any protocols or safety measures that would allow TMS with DBS in place?

3. **Would you be willing to see me clinically?** Even outside of a trial setting, a consultation with a Center of Excellence for dystonia would be immensely valuable. I have not had access to a movement disorder specialist with dystonia-specific expertise since my DBS surgery.

4. **Are there other trials at Duke I should be aware of?** Given my treatment-resistant case and existing DBS, I am open to any research opportunities that might apply.

I am highly motivated, willing to travel to Durham for appointments, and happy to provide my full medical records and imaging. I have done extensive research on my condition and treatment options and am looking for a physician and research team who can partner with me on a comprehensive, evidence-based treatment plan.
