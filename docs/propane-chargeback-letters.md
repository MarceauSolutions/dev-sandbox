## Merchant's Terms — Why They Don't Protect Them

### Their Dispute Resolution Clause
They require a 3-step process (written notice → negotiation → CEDR mediation) before any chargeback. They claim skipping this = material breach + forfeiture of refund + liability for their legal costs.

**Why it doesn't apply:**
- While a terms link was sent at 15:27 on March 2, payment links followed just 2 minutes later at 15:29 — no reasonable time to review. No formal acceptance mechanism was provided before payment. The merchant's own process required a separate Jotform signature, completed March 5 — 3 days after payment. You never formally agreed to this clause before the transaction.
- A chargeback is a banking process between you and Amex, not a legal proceeding. Their contract cannot override your rights with your card issuer.
- Amex is not a party to their terms and will evaluate the dispute independently.

### Their Cooling-Off Waiver
They claim accessing materials waives the 14-day statutory cooling-off period.

**Why it doesn't apply:**
- Under UK Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013, the waiver must be explicitly consented to BEFORE access is granted, which requires formally agreed terms BEFORE payment. A link sent 2 minutes before payment links — with no acceptance mechanism — does not constitute explicit consent. The formal agreement (Jotform) wasn't signed until March 5, after access was already granted. They did it backwards.
- Even under their own waiver, the service delivered (group chat) is materially different from what was sold (1-on-1 Zoom coaching), which is grounds for rescission regardless of cooling-off period.

### Their Collections Threat
They claim filing a chargeback triggers account removal and potential collections referral.

**Why it's empty:**
- They'd have to prove you owe them money. Given terms were signed post-payment, the core service was misrepresented, and you're within their own 14-day window, any collections claim would fail.
- A UK company pursuing a US consumer through collections for a disputed charge is extremely costly and rarely pursued.
- Amex handles the dispute process — if the chargeback succeeds, the merchant has no valid debt to collect.
