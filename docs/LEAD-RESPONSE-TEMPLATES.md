# Lead Response Templates

*Last Updated: 2026-01-22*

## Principles

1. **Ask before pitching** - Understand their pain first
2. **Be specific** - Reference their business, not generic benefits
3. **Be honest** - Don't oversell capabilities
4. **Offer value** - Lead with something free/helpful
5. **One clear CTA** - Don't overwhelm with options

## Our Specialty (Emphasize This)

**We work closely with clients to figure out exactly what they need - and fill gaps they don't even know exist yet.**

This is our differentiator:
- We're not pushing a single product
- We diagnose before we prescribe
- We build custom solutions, not cookie-cutter templates
- We're honest when something isn't the right fit

**How to communicate this:**
- "I help businesses find where they're leaking time and money, then fix it"
- "Most of my clients didn't know what they needed until we talked"
- "I'm not here to sell you something - I'm here to figure out if I can actually help"

## Hot Lead Follow-Up (Responding to Interest)

### When They Ask "What Do You Do?"

```
Good question! Short version: I build AI-powered systems that handle the repetitive stuff so you can focus on what matters.

For a spa like yours, that could mean:
- An AI assistant that handles booking inquiries via text
- Automated follow-up with leads who don't book
- A system that re-engages past clients for repeat visits

But honestly, the best way to know what'd help is a quick call. What's eating up your time right now?
```

### When They're Confused (Like Inhale Exhale)

```
Ha, fair question - my first message was confusing. Let me be clearer:

I'm not selling websites. I help businesses automate the annoying stuff - like following up with leads, answering common questions, booking appointments automatically.

For wellness/spa businesses specifically, the biggest wins I've seen are:
1. AI that responds to inquiries 24/7 so you never miss a lead
2. Automatic rebooking reminders to past clients
3. Review request systems that run on autopilot

Would any of those actually be useful for you? If not, I'll leave you alone!
```

### When They Show Budget Concerns

```
Totally get it - AI sounds expensive. Here's the reality:

Most of what I do costs between $200-500 to set up, then $50-200/month to run. Compare that to hiring someone part-time or paying a marketing agency.

The question is whether the time/leads you're losing is worth more than that. Happy to do a quick analysis if you want - no pitch, just an honest look at what's costing you.
```

## Pain Point Specific Responses

### "I'm Too Busy / Overwhelmed"

```
That's exactly what I hear most. Here's usually what's eating the time:

1. Answering the same questions over and over
2. Following up with leads manually
3. Booking/scheduling back-and-forth
4. Admin stuff that should just happen automatically

Which of those sounds most like your situation? I can give you a specific idea of what we could automate.
```

### "I Need More Leads/Customers"

```
Getting more leads is actually the easy part - the hard part is following up with them consistently.

I can help with both:
- Lead generation: Finding people who need your services
- Lead conversion: Making sure every inquiry gets a response and follow-up

Most businesses I work with are sitting on leads they never properly followed up with. Want me to take a look at your current system?
```

### "I'm Missing Calls"

```
Missed calls are brutal - studies show 80% of callers won't leave a voicemail and won't call back.

Options to fix this:
1. AI phone system that answers, qualifies, and books (honest caveat: not as human-sounding as a real person yet)
2. Instant text-back when you miss a call: "Sorry I missed you! I'm [busy/in session]. Can I call you back in [X]?"
3. Online booking so they don't need to call at all

What's your current setup for handling calls when you're busy?
```

### "I Have a Website But..."

```
Websites are tricky - having one isn't enough if it's not converting visitors to customers.

Quick wins I usually see:
1. Adding a simple chat/text widget (captures leads who won't fill out forms)
2. Adding social proof (reviews, testimonials)
3. Clearer call-to-action (book now vs. "contact us")

But sometimes the website isn't the problem - it's what happens AFTER someone inquires. Want me to take a quick look?
```

## Objection Handling

### "I'm not interested"

```
No problem! If anything changes or you want a second opinion on your systems, I'm around. Have a good one!
```
(Don't push - respect the no)

### "This sounds like a scam"

```
Ha, fair - cold texts are sketchy. I'm a real person in [City]. Here's my LinkedIn: [link]. My website: marceausolutions.com.

I reach out to local businesses because that's who I work with. No pressure - just thought I'd see if what I do could help.
```

### "How much does it cost?"

```
Depends on what you need:
- One-time setups: $200-1000
- Ongoing services: $50-300/month
- Lead lists: $150 for 100 qualified contacts

What problem are you trying to solve? I can give you a specific number.
```

### "I already have someone for that"

```
Nice! Out of curiosity, what are they handling for you? Always interested in what's working for local businesses.

If you're ever looking for a second opinion or want to compare, let me know. Otherwise, sounds like you're set!
```

## Notes for William

**Things to avoid:**
- Leading with "voice AI" or any single product
- Promising results we can't guarantee
- Being pushy after a "no"
- Using jargon (AI, automation, etc.) without explaining value

**Things to always do:**
- Ask about THEIR situation first
- Be honest about what works and what doesn't
- Offer value before asking for anything
- Make it easy to say no (reduces friction)
