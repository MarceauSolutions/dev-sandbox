## Market Read (SW FL, May 2026)

The local market for I&E/SCADA techs at 0–3 years experience is thin but real. Lee County Utilities is running three concurrent SCADA/controls postings right now; Manatee County's top salary of $41/hr is the best compensation signal on the board for someone at William's level; Charlotte County has had a continuous open for 15 months — all within commutable range of Naples. The practical constraint is that nearly every posting is titled "Senior" with a 4-year preference. William's combination of Collier County + FGCU TA is an argument — not a guarantee — to clear that bar.

For material salary upside ($80k+), the Tampa Bay corridor is where density concentrates: Hillsborough County, City of Tampa, Pinellas utilities, and TECO all post I&C roles in the $60–90k range, but they want journeyman time and prefer utility-sector experience over water/wastewater backgrounds. The cleanest path is take the Manatee or Lee County Senior role now ($57–86k ceiling), stack 2 more years of utility SCADA against the Allen-Bradley/lift station foundation already in place, then step up to Tampa Bay or a system integrator at $90–110k.

SFWMD is the overlooked wild card: competitive pay, 4-day week, genuine field SCADA work — and the minimum requirements are the most accessible on this list. The West Palm base is the only real obstacle. FPL/NextEra Naples activity is inconclusive from public search; their I&C pipeline gravitates toward Turkey Point and Riviera Beach. The private engineering firms (B&V, AECOM, Carollo) are not posting tech-level work in Florida right now — that segment will open up when capital project pipelines accelerate.
