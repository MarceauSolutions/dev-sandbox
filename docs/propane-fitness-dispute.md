   | **Written (Jim's email, 15:18 — exact)** | £7,875 | £6,300 |

   Jim quoted a retail price approximately **37% higher** on the call than what he wrote in his own follow-up email minutes later (£10,812.50 vs £7,875). The math is internally consistent (both apply exactly 20%), meaning this was not a slip — he deliberately used different base figures on the call to inflate the perceived value of the discount.

   **NOTE:** Verbal figures are from memory — do not state specific numbers in formal correspondence. The email figures are exact and verifiable. The discrepancy argument stands on Jim's own written words alone.

2. **Jim stated the price was non-negotiable — this was a lie.** When William asked if the price was negotiable, Jim said no. However, the discrepancy between what he quoted verbally and what he put in writing proves the price was already being manipulated. A non-negotiable price does not change between a phone call and a follow-up email sent minutes later. This is a textbook high-pressure sales tactic.

   - 15:18 — Sales email with price terms sent

4. **No USD equivalent was disclosed.** The program is priced in GBP. William is a US customer. The USD equivalent ($8,511.94) was never stated in any email. The exchange rate impact was not communicated.
