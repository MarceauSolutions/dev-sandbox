## Key Facts Supporting the Dispute

1. **Jim misrepresented the pricing on the call.** During the March 2, 2026 call, Jim verbally quoted a list price and a 20% discounted price that were materially higher than the figures in his own written follow-up email sent minutes later:

   | | List Price | After 20% Discount |
   |--|------------|-------------------|
   | **Verbal (on call — from memory)** | ~£10,812.50 | ~£8,650 |
   | **Written (Jim's email, 15:18 — exact)** | £7,875 | £6,300 |

   Jim quoted a retail price approximately **37% higher** on the call than what he wrote in his own follow-up email minutes later (£10,812.50 vs £7,875). The math is internally consistent (both apply exactly 20%), meaning this was not a slip — he deliberately used different base figures on the call to inflate the perceived value of the discount.

   **NOTE:** Verbal figures are from memory — do not state specific numbers in formal correspondence. The email figures are exact and verifiable. The discrepancy argument stands on Jim's own written words alone.

2. **Jim stated the price was non-negotiable — this was a lie.** When William asked if the price was negotiable, Jim said no. However, the discrepancy between what he quoted verbally and what he put in writing proves the price was already being manipulated. A non-negotiable price does not change between a phone call and a follow-up email sent minutes later. This is a textbook high-pressure sales tactic.

2. **Payment was rushed.** Timeline on March 2:
   - 15:18 — Sales email with price terms sent
   - 15:27 — Terms link sent (9 min after price email)
   - 15:29 — First payment link sent (2 min after terms)
   - 15:30 — Second payment link sent (1 min later)
   - 15:32 — First charge processed (2 min after second link)
   - 15:40 — Second charge processed
   - Total time from price email to full payment: **~22 minutes**

3. **Terms were signed AFTER payment.** The signed invoice (JotForm) is dated March 5 — 3 days after £6,300 was already charged.

4. **No USD equivalent was disclosed.** The program is priced in GBP. William is a US customer. The USD equivalent ($8,511.94) was never stated in any email. The exchange rate impact was not communicated.

5. **Course access was not provided immediately — breach of written promise.** Jim's own sales email (15:18, March 2) explicitly states: *"Once you've processed the payment you'll receive immediate access via email with login information and next steps."* Access was NOT provided immediately. It took a full day of back-and-forth to resolve. This is a direct breach of a specific written representation made at point of sale.

6. **Foreign transaction fees charged — exact amounts confirmed.** As a US customer paying in GBP, foreign transaction fees were applied by Amex on both cards:
   - Card ending 1007: $4,255.97 charge + $114.91 foreign transaction fee
   - Card ending 1049: $4,255.97 charge + $114.91 foreign transaction fee

   - **Grand total actually paid: $8,741.76 USD** (not $8,511.94 — an additional $229.82 was taken without any disclosure)
   These fees were never mentioned by Propane at any point before or during payment.

7. **The payment links describe the charge differently.** The SamCart URL says "2-x-3150" — two equal payments of £3,150. This supports the interpretation that the total was split across two cards, not that William independently agreed to two £3,150 charges.
