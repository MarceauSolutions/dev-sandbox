# Market Size Analysis: DecideForHer

**Agent 1 | Market Viability Research**
**Date:** 2026-01-18

---

## Executive Summary

The "DecideForHer" concept sits at the intersection of three markets: food delivery ($350B+), meal planning apps, and couples/relationship apps. While the TAM is massive, the realistic serviceable market for a niche "couples food decision" app is narrow and problematic. Key findings reveal **significant market viability concerns** including: unclear monetization path, potential brand perception issues, and an oversaturated adjacent market.

**Bottom Line: The market opportunity exists, but the positioning is risky and the SOM is severely limited by both technical and social factors.**

### Willingness to Pay for Decision Apps

**Consumer App Pricing Reality:**

|--------------|---------------|-----------------|

| Couples apps (Paired, Lasting) | $10-15/month | 2-5% |

### SAM Calculation

**Conservative SAM Methodology:**

```
25 million couples (US, tech-savvy, order delivery)
x 10% might consider a decision app = 2.5 million
x 3% would actually pay = 75,000 paying couples
x $60/year (optimistic annual spend) = $4.5 million SAM
```

**Aggressive SAM Methodology:**

```
80 million US couples
x 30% order delivery regularly = 24 million
x 5% would consider app = 1.2 million
x 5% would pay = 60,000 paying
x $120/year = $7.2 million SAM
```

**SAM Conclusion:** **$4-8 million annually** (US market only)

**Critical Caveat:** This assumes people would pay for a decision app. In reality, most couples would view this as a "nice to have" that should be free.

## Key Trends Affecting This Market

### Positive Trends

1. **Food Delivery is Habitual**
   - 60% of US consumers order delivery at least once a week (2024)
   - Average US household spends $67/week on food delivery
   - Post-pandemic habits have stuck
   - Source: National Restaurant Association, DoorDash earnings

2. **AI Personalization is Expected**
   - 70% of consumers expect personalized recommendations
   - Machine learning in food apps is table stakes
   - "For You" recommendations drive 35% of DoorDash orders
   - Source: McKinsey, DoorDash investor presentations

3. **Couples Apps Growing (Slowly)**
   - Paired app: 5M+ users, raised $12M Series A
   - Lasting: Focus on relationship wellness, 1.5M+ users
   - Indicates willingness to pay for relationship tools
   - Source: Crunchbase, App Store

4. **Decision Fatigue is Real**
   - 57% of couples report food decisions as source of minor conflict
   - "Where do you want to eat?" is documented relationship friction
   - Source: YouGov surveys, relationship research

### Negative Trends (Critical)

1. **Native AI Recommendations Eating the Market**
   - DoorDash "Personalized Recommendations" launched 2024
   - Uber Eats AI-powered menu suggestions
   - Grubhub "Order Again" and "Recommended For You"
   - **Major platforms already solving this problem**
   - Source: Platform press releases, app updates

2. **Consumer App Monetization Crisis**
   - Subscription fatigue at all-time high
   - Ad-supported models require massive scale
   - In-app purchases <$5 are expectation
   - Source: Sensor Tower, Apptopia

3. **Privacy Concerns with Predictive Data**
   - "Cycle timing" tracking is sensitive (see: Period tracker backlash)
   - Regulatory risk (GDPR, state privacy laws)
   - User trust issues with relationship data
   - Source: Pew Research, FTC guidance

4. **Gendered Marketing Backlash Risk**
   - "DecideForHer" framing could be seen as patronizing
   - Social media criticism risk (viral negative PR)
   - Limits addressable market to hetero couples
   - May alienate progressive demographics (who skew tech-savvy)

5. **Super-App Threat**
   - If DoorDash/Uber add "couples mode" or shared profiles
   - Partners already adding family/household features
   - No moat for standalone decision app

### Overall Market Size Score

## 2.0 / 5 Stars

**Rationale:**

The market size analysis reveals a **deceptively attractive TAM** that masks serious structural problems:

**Problems:**

1. **SAM is tiny**: Despite $350B food delivery TAM, the realistic market for a standalone couples decision app is $4-8M max

2. **SOM is non-viable**: Year 1 revenue of $3-9K doesn't justify development time

3. **Platforms already solving this**: DoorDash, Uber Eats have AI recommendations built-in. Why download separate app?

4. **No moat**: Any platform can add "partner preferences" feature tomorrow

5. **Brand positioning risk**: "DecideForHer" name:
   - Excludes 50% of couples (same-sex, role-reversed)
   - Risks social media backlash
   - Plays into stereotypes that may alienate target demo

6. **Privacy concerns**: Tracking "cycle timing" to predict food preferences:
   - Invites regulatory scrutiny
   - User trust issues
   - Post-Roe sensitivity around period tracking

7. **Consumer app graveyard**: 90% of indie consumer apps fail. No evidence this category is different.

**What Would Need to Be True for Success:**

- Millions in marketing budget to build brand
- Partnership with major delivery platform (unlikely - they'd build it themselves)
- Pivot away from gendered positioning
- Find sustainable monetization beyond subscriptions
- Beat platforms at AI personalization (very hard)

## Recommendations

### If Proceeding (Not Recommended Based on Market Size):

1. **Rebrand immediately**: "DecideForHer" is brand liability
   - Consider: "WhatToEat", "FoodMate", "OrderTogether"
   - Gender-neutral positioning

2. **Pivot to B2B**: Sell to restaurants/delivery platforms as feature
   - "Couples mode" white-label solution
   - $50K-100K per platform license vs $5/user

3. **Consider freemium ad model**:
   - Free app, restaurant-sponsored recommendations
   - Affiliate revenue from orders placed

4. **Drop the "cycle tracking"**:
   - Privacy risk not worth marginal prediction improvement
   - Focus on order history, time patterns, stated preferences

### Alternative Opportunities in This Space:

1. **Restaurant-side AI**: Help restaurants predict what couples will order (B2B)
2. **Delivery platform integration**: Build for DoorDash API, not standalone
3. **Group ordering enhancement**: Solve for families/roommates, not just couples

## Conclusion

**The market size appears attractive at the TAM level but collapses at the SAM/SOM level.**

This is a classic "big market, tiny niche" trap:
- Food delivery is $350B
- Couples apps are $200-400M
- Couples food decision apps are...basically $0 (no proven market)

The fundamental question: **Would you download and pay for an app that does what "just ask your partner" does for free?**

Market data suggests: Probably not in enough numbers to build a viable business.
