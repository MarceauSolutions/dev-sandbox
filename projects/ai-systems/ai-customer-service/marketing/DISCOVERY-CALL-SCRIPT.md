# Voice AI Discovery Call Script

**Goal:** Qualify prospect, understand pain points, demonstrate value, book follow-up or send proposal

**Duration:** 20-30 minutes

---

## Pre-Call Prep (5 minutes before call)

**Research the prospect:**
- [ ] Visit their website
- [ ] Call their business number (see how they answer)
- [ ] Read Google reviews (look for phone-related complaints)
- [ ] Check their hours/services
- [ ] Note any obvious pain points

**Have ready:**
- [ ] Case study (HVAC-CASE-STUDY.md)
- [ ] Pricing sheet
- [ ] Calendar for demo scheduling
- [ ] Notepad for notes

---

## Opening (2 minutes)

**Your Intro:**
> "Hi [Name], this is William from Marceau Solutions. Thanks for taking the time to chat today. Did I catch you at a good time?"

*[Wait for response]*

> "Great. Just so you know, this call will take about 20 minutes. I want to learn about your business, see if Voice AI might be a good fit, and if it is, show you a quick demo or send you some information. Sound good?"

**Their Context:**
> "Before we dive in, can you tell me a little about your business? What do you do, how many people on your team, and what's your service area?"

*[Take notes]*

---

## Discovery Questions (10 minutes)

### 1. Current Phone Situation

**Question:**
> "Walk me through what happens when a customer calls your business right now. Who answers?"

**Listen for:**
- Office manager/receptionist (overwhelmed?)
- Technicians/staff (taking them away from work?)
- Voicemail (how often? how quickly do they call back?)
- Owner answering (shouldn't they be focusing on growth?)

**Follow-up:**
> "What percentage of calls would you say you miss or goes to voicemail during busy times?"

*[If they say <10%, Voice AI might not be urgent. If >20%, strong pain point]*

---

### 2. After-Hours & Emergencies

**Question:**
> "What happens when someone calls at 7 PM or on a Sunday? Do you have after-hours coverage?"

**Listen for:**
- No coverage = lost revenue
- Answering service = inconsistent quality
- Owner always on-call = burned out

**Pain amplification:**
> "How many emergency calls do you think you miss per month after hours? What's the average value of an emergency service call?"

*[Do the math: 5 missed calls × $500 = $2,500/month lost = $30K/year]*

---

### 3. Lead Capture & Follow-Up

**Question:**
> "When someone calls with a question or wants a quote, how do you capture their information and make sure you follow up?"

**Listen for:**
- Manual entry (prone to errors)
- No CRM (losing track of leads)
- Slow follow-up (competitors beating them)

**Follow-up:**
> "How quickly do you typically get back to new leads?"

*[If >2 hours, point out: "Studies show you lose 80% of leads if you don't respond within the first hour."]*

---

### 4. Appointment Scheduling

**Question:**
> "How does appointment scheduling work? Can customers book directly, or do they have to wait for a callback?"

**Listen for:**
- Phone tag (inefficient)
- Double-bookings (frustrating)
- No online booking (losing tech-savvy customers)

---

### 5. Staff Time & Overhead

**Question:**
> "How much time does your team spend on the phone each day answering basic questions like 'What are your hours?' or 'Do you service my area?'"

**Listen for:**
- Office manager spending 50%+ time on phones
- Technicians distracted by calls
- Expensive employee time on low-value tasks

**ROI setup:**
> "If I could free up [X] hours per week for your team, what would they do with that time? More jobs? Better customer service? Business development?"

---

### 6. Budget & Decision-Making

**Question:**
> "Have you looked into phone answering solutions before? What stopped you from implementing something?"

**Listen for:**
- Answering services too expensive or bad quality
- Tried hiring, but hard to find good people
- "Too complicated" = need simple solution

**Budget qualifier:**
> "Just so I know we're in the right ballpark - our Voice AI systems typically run $5K-10K for setup and $150-300/month ongoing. Does that fit within your budget for solving this problem?"

*[If yes, continue. If no, ask what their budget is]*

---

## Pain Summary & Value Proposition (3 minutes)

**Summarize what you heard:**
> "Okay, so let me make sure I understand:
> - You're missing [X%] of calls during busy times
> - You have no after-hours coverage, losing [Y] emergency calls/month
> - Your [office manager/staff] spends [Z] hours/week on repetitive questions
> - Follow-up takes [A] hours, and you're losing leads to faster competitors
>
> Does that sound right?"

*[Wait for confirmation]*

**Introduce Voice AI:**
> "Here's how Voice AI solves all of that:
>
> **24/7 Coverage:** Every call gets answered in under 2 rings, even at 3 AM.
>
> **Instant Answers:** Common questions (hours, pricing, service area) answered immediately without tying up your staff.
>
> **Appointment Booking:** Customers can book on the spot. No phone tag. Confirmations sent automatically.
>
> **Emergency Routing:** AI recognizes urgent situations and immediately transfers to your on-call tech.
>
> **Lead Capture:** Every caller's info is logged in your CRM automatically. Hot leads sent to your phone via SMS in real-time.
>
> **Natural Voice:** Sounds human. Most customers don't even realize it's AI."

## Objection Handling

### Objection: "That sounds expensive"

**Response:**
> "I understand. Let's look at it this way:
>
> You said you're missing [X] calls per month. If even half of those are $500 jobs, that's $[X × $250] in lost revenue per month = $[X × $3,000] per year.
>
> Voice AI costs $[setup] one-time + $[monthly] per month = $[total year 1].
>
> So if it captures just [breakeven number] extra jobs in the first year, it pays for itself. Everything after that is pure profit.
>
> Does that make sense?"

---

### Objection: "Our customers won't like talking to AI"

**Response:**
> "That's a common concern, but here's what we've found:
>
> 1. **Most customers don't realize it's AI** - It sounds that natural
> 2. **They prefer instant answers** over waiting on hold or getting voicemail
> 3. **Emergency calls transfer to a real person** immediately
>
> And here's the thing: Right now, when they call and get voicemail, they're not getting ANY service. AI answering immediately is a massive upgrade from that.
>
> Would you rather they talk to AI and get help, or call your competitor instead?"

---

### Objection: "We tried an answering service before and it was terrible"

**Response:**
> "I hear you - traditional answering services are hit or miss. Here's the difference:
>
> **Answering services:**
> - Different person every time
> - Reading from a script
> - Can't book appointments
> - Expensive ($500-1000/month ongoing)
>
> **Voice AI:**
> - Same 'voice' every time (consistency)
> - Actually understands your business
> - Books appointments automatically
> - Lower monthly cost ($150-300/month)
>
> It's a completely different experience. That's why I want to show you a demo - once you hear it, you'll understand the difference."

---

### Objection: "I need to think about it"

**Response:**
> "Absolutely, this is a business decision and you should think it through.
>
> Can I ask - what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the price, whether it'll work for your business, or something else?
>
> *[Address their specific concern]*
>
> Here's what I'll do: I'll send you the case study and a proposal today. Let's schedule a 15-minute follow-up call for [next week] to go through any questions. Does [day/time] work?"

---

### Objection: "I need to talk to my partner/spouse first"

**Response:**
> "Of course. Would it make sense for them to jump on a quick call with us? Or would you prefer I send you the materials and you two can review together?
>
> Either way, I'll put together a proposal so you have everything you need to make a decision."

## Success Metrics

**Track for each call:**
- [ ] Prospect qualified (budget, pain, authority, timeline)
- [ ] Pain points identified (list top 3)
- [ ] Demo shown (yes/no)
- [ ] Proposal promised (date)
- [ ] Follow-up scheduled (date/time)
- [ ] Objections raised (list)
- [ ] Outcome (proposal sent, demo scheduled, not interested, follow-up needed)

**Goal conversion rates:**
- Discovery call → Proposal sent: 80%
- Proposal sent → Follow-up call: 60%
- Follow-up call → Closed deal: 30-40%
- **Overall discovery → closed: 15-25%**

---

## Tips for Success

**Do:**
- ✅ Listen more than you talk (70/30 rule)
- ✅ Ask open-ended questions ("Tell me about..." vs "Do you...?")
- ✅ Quantify pain ($X lost per month)
- ✅ Use the case study (social proof works)
- ✅ Offer demo when they're engaged
- ✅ Schedule follow-up before ending call

**Don't:**
- ❌ Pitch features without understanding pain first
- ❌ Overwhelm with technical details
- ❌ Bad-mouth competitors or answering services
- ❌ Give discounts in first call (hold your price)
- ❌ Let them "think about it" without scheduling follow-up

---

**Remember:** You're not selling Voice AI. You're solving their problem of missed revenue from unanswered calls. Lead with value, not features.
