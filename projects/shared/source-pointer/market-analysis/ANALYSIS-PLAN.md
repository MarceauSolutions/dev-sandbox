# Market Viability Analysis: Source Pointer

## Product Hypothesis

**What it is**: An AI assistant for AI skeptics that points users to authoritative sources instead of synthesizing answers, with deep-linking to the exact section/paragraph where the information is located.

**Who it's for**:
- AI skeptics who don't trust AI-generated content
- Researchers who need to cite sources
- Journalists fact-checking information
- Students who need to verify claims
- Professionals in regulated industries (legal, medical, financial)

**Problem it solves**:
- Distrust of AI-generated answers (hallucinations, no sources)
- Time wasted scrolling through long documents to find relevant sections
- Need to verify information before acting on it
- Citation requirements for academic/professional work

**How we'd make money**: TBD (subscription, usage-based, enterprise)

## Agent 1: Market Size Questions
1. What is the TAM for AI-powered research/search tools?
2. What is the SAM for "trust and verify" or source-citation tools?
3. What percentage of users distrust AI-generated content (surveys, studies)?
4. Is the AI skeptic market growing or shrinking?
5. What industries have the highest demand for verifiable, sourced information?

## Agent 2: Competition Questions
1. What tools currently provide source citations with AI? (Perplexity, You.com, Bing Chat)
2. How do current tools handle deep-linking to specific sections?
3. What's the gap in existing solutions for AI skeptics?
4. What browser extensions or tools help with research verification?
5. What do competitors charge and what's their business model?
6. Is there a product specifically marketed to AI skeptics?

## Agent 3: Customer Research Questions
1. Who exactly is an "AI skeptic" - demographics, industries, pain points?
2. How painful is the problem of verifying AI-generated information (1-10)?
3. What workarounds do AI skeptics currently use?
4. Where do AI skeptics congregate online (forums, subreddits, communities)?
5. What would trigger an AI skeptic to trust a tool like this?
6. What's the difference between "AI skeptic" and "AI avoider"?

## Agent 4: Monetization Questions
1. What pricing model works for research/productivity tools?
2. What price point would AI skeptics pay for verified sourcing?
3. B2B vs B2C - which is more viable?
4. What are realistic customer acquisition costs for this audience?
5. Are there enterprise use cases (legal, compliance, journalism)?
6. What adjacent revenue opportunities exist (API, integrations)?

## Evaluation Criteria
- **Feasibility**: Can this actually be built?
- **Market Size**: Is there a real market for AI skeptics?
- **Competition**: Is the gap real or is it already solved?
- **Customer Pain**: How badly do they need this?
- **Monetization**: Can this make money?

## Scoring System
- 5 stars: Exceptional - strong signal to proceed
- 4 stars: Good - worth pursuing
- 3 stars: Acceptable - proceed with caution
- 2 stars: Poor - significant concerns
- 1 star: Unusable - likely not viable
