## Key Market Trends

### 1. AI Hallucination Awareness Growing
- High-profile cases (lawyers citing fake cases, medical misinformation) driving demand for verification
- Google's AI Overviews controversy (2024) highlighted accuracy concerns
- Media coverage of AI errors increasing public skepticism
- **Implication:** Tailwind for source-first tools

### 2. Regulatory Pressure for AI Transparency
- EU AI Act (2024): Requires transparency in AI-generated content
- Proposed US legislation on AI disclosure
- Academic institutions updating AI citation policies
- **Implication:** Compliance driver for enterprise adoption

### 3. "Trust but Verify" Becoming Standard Practice
- Professionals developing AI verification workflows
- Rise of AI fact-checking as a service
- Integration of citation features in mainstream AI (Perplexity, Claude, Copilot)
- **Implication:** Market validation, but also increasing competition

### 4. Perplexity's Success Validates Model
- Perplexity AI valued at $520M+ (2024), growing rapidly
- Proves users will pay for AI with sources
- However: Perplexity synthesizes then cites vs. Source Pointer's "point first" model
- **Implication:** Validated market, differentiation opportunity

### 5. Enterprise Demand for Auditable AI
- Large organizations need audit trails for AI-assisted decisions
- Liability concerns driving policy changes
- IT procurement requiring "explainable AI"
- **Implication:** Enterprise tier potential with source logging features

### 6. Academic Citation Standards Evolving
- Universities creating AI citation policies
- Need for tools that support proper attribution
- Integration with reference managers (Zotero, EndNote, Mendeley)
- **Implication:** Academic vertical ripe for targeting
