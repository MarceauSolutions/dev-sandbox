# Workflow: Market Research Interview

**Created:** 2026-01-19
**Purpose:** Conduct structured market research interviews with potential clients to validate niche selection and product-market fit
**Output:** Interview transcript, analysis summary, and niche decision recommendation

---

## Overview

This workflow guides systematic market research interviews to validate business ideas, understand customer pain points, and make data-driven niche selection decisions. Uses "Mom Test" principles (ask about past behavior, not future hypotheticals) to get truthful insights.

---

## Use Cases

- Considering a new niche (e.g., "Should we focus on restaurants vs gyms?")
- Validating a product idea before building (e.g., "Do gyms actually need automated booking?")
- Understanding pricing expectations for a new market segment
- Identifying which pain points are most severe (prioritization)
- Deciding between multiple feature sets for MVP

---

## Prerequisites

Before conducting interviews:
- ✅ Target market hypothesis defined (e.g., "Naples restaurants without websites")
- ✅ Product/service concept drafted (what you might build)
- ✅ 5-10 potential interviewees identified (target customers)
- ✅ Interview questions prepared (see script below)
- ✅ Tracking spreadsheet created (see template)

---

## The "Mom Test" Principles

**Foundation:** Don't ask hypothetical questions ("Would you buy this?"). Ask about past behavior and current pain.

| ❌ Bad Question | ✅ Good Question |
|----------------|-----------------|
| "Would you use an automated booking system?" | "How do you currently handle appointment booking? Walk me through the last time a customer booked." |
| "Would you pay $500/month for this?" | "What do you currently spend on [similar solutions]? Show me the invoice." |
| "Do you think this is a good idea?" | "When's the last time [problem] happened? What did you do?" |
| "How much would you pay for this?" | "What's the most expensive tool you bought this year for [category]? Why?" |

**Key Rules:**
1. **Talk about their life, not your idea** - Focus 80% on their problems, 20% on your solution
2. **Ask about specifics in the past** - "Last time this happened..." not "What if..."
3. **Talk less, listen more** - Your job is to learn, not pitch
4. **Ask for commitment before revealing your idea** - "If I built this, would you be my first customer?" (if yes, they'll ask for details)

## Interview Script (10 Core Questions)

**Context Setting (1 min):**
```
"Hi [Name], thanks for taking 20 minutes with me. I'm researching [industry]
to understand what's working and what's broken in [category]. I'm NOT selling
anything today - just trying to learn from operators like you. Sound good?"
```

---

### **Section 1: Current State (Questions 1-3)**

**Objective:** Understand current workflows and pain points

**Q1: Current Process**
> "Walk me through how you currently handle [process - e.g., customer orders, appointment booking, etc.].
> Start from when a customer first contacts you."

**What to listen for:**
- Manual steps (opportunities for automation)
- Frustration points ("This is a pain because...")
- Workarounds ("We have to...")
- Tools they currently use

**Follow-up:**
- "How much time does this take per day/week?"
- "Who on your team handles this?"
- "Show me [the tool/spreadsheet/system you use]" (if possible)

---

**Q2: Recent Pain Point**
> "Think about the last time [problem] happened. Walk me through exactly what happened,
> what you did, and how long it took to fix."

**What to listen for:**
- Frequency (how often does this problem occur?)
- Severity (how painful was it? money lost? time wasted?)
- Current solution (how do they handle it now?)

**Follow-up:**
- "How often does this happen?" (daily, weekly, monthly?)
- "What did it cost you?" (time, money, customers)
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would have prevented this?"

---

**Q3: Current Tools & Costs**
> "What tools/software do you currently pay for to handle [category]?"
> "Can you show me a recent invoice or ballpark what you spend per month?"

**What to listen for:**
- Exact tools used (competitors/alternatives)
- Monthly costs (price anchoring)
- What they like/hate about current tools

**Follow-up:**
- "What's the most expensive tool you use? Why do you pay for it?"
- "What tools did you try and cancel? Why?"
- "If cost wasn't an issue, what would you change?"

---

### **Section 2: Pain Severity (Questions 4-6)**

**Objective:** Quantify pain and prioritize problems

**Q4: Biggest Frustration**
> "If you could fix ONE thing about [category/process], what would it be?"

**What to listen for:**
- Top-of-mind pain (most severe)
- Why it's the biggest issue (impact on business)

**Follow-up:**
- "Why is that the top priority vs [other issue they mentioned]?"
- "Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?"

---

**Q5: Cost of Inaction**
> "What does [problem] cost you right now? Think time, money, lost customers, stress."

**What to listen for:**
- Quantified impact ("We lose about 20 orders a month")
- Opportunity cost ("I spend 10 hours/week on this instead of growing the business")
- Customer experience impact ("Customers complain about...")

**Follow-up:**
- "How did you calculate that number?" (verify it's real, not guessed)
- "Is this getting better, worse, or staying the same?"

---

**Q6: Attempted Solutions**
> "What have you already tried to solve [problem]? What worked? What didn't?"

**What to listen for:**
- Failed solutions (avoid building what didn't work)
- Budget allocated to solving this (willingness to pay)
- Why previous attempts failed (feature requirements)

**Follow-up:**
- "Why did you stop using [previous solution]?"
- "What would it need to have done differently for you to keep it?"

---

### **Section 3: Buying Behavior (Questions 7-9)**

**Objective:** Understand how they make purchasing decisions

**Q7: Recent Purchase**
> "What's the last business tool/software you bought? Walk me through the decision process
> from 'I have a problem' to 'I'm paying for this.'"

**What to listen for:**
- Decision timeline (how long from problem → purchase?)
- Decision makers (who needs to approve?)
- Evaluation criteria (price, features, reviews, trial?)

**Follow-up:**
- "How long did it take from first hearing about it to buying?"
- "Did you try competitors? Why did you choose this one?"
- "Who else was involved in the decision?"

---

**Q8: Budget & ROI Expectations**
> "For tools in this category, what's a reasonable monthly cost? What would feel expensive?"

**What to listen for:**
- Price anchoring (what they consider "normal" vs "expensive")
- ROI expectations ("It would need to save me X hours to be worth it")

**Follow-up:**
- "What would this tool need to do to justify [price point]?"
- "Have you ever paid more than [their stated range]? For what?"

---

**Q9: Decision Triggers**
> "What would need to happen for you to actually buy a solution for [problem] in the next 30 days?"

**What to listen for:**
- Urgency (is this a "nice to have" or "must have"?)
- Triggers ("If we hire one more person..." "If we lose one more customer...")
- Budget cycles ("We review tools in Q1...")

**Follow-up:**
- "Has that trigger happened before? What did you do then?"
- "If I had a solution ready today, would you want to see it?"

---

### **Section 4: Solution Validation (Question 10)**

**Objective:** Test if they'd actually buy (commitment test)

**Q10: Commitment Test**
> "Based on what you've told me, I might be able to help with [specific pain point].
> If I built something that [solves specific problem in specific way], would you want
> to be one of the first 5 customers? It would mean giving feedback and maybe a small discount."

**What to listen for:**
- Immediate "YES" = strong signal (real pain)
- "Maybe" or "I'd have to see it" = weak signal (nice-to-have)
- "NO" or deflection = not a real pain OR you misunderstood the problem

**Follow-up (if YES):**
- "What would success look like for you? How would you measure it?"
- "Can I follow up in 2 weeks with a prototype?"
- "Who else should I talk to in your industry?"

**Follow-up (if NO):**
- "What did I miss? What would make this a no-brainer for you?"
- "Is this even a problem worth solving?"

---

**Closing (1 min):**
```
"This was super helpful. Last question: who else in [industry] should I talk to
who deals with [problem]? I'm trying to talk to 10 people this week."
```

(Get 2-3 referrals to snowball interviews)

## Steps

### Step 1: Identify Interview Candidates

**Objective:** Find 5-10 potential customers to interview

**Actions:**
1. Define target persona:
   - Industry (e.g., restaurants, gyms, home services)
   - Size (revenue, employees, locations)
   - Geography (local, national, international)
   - Pain point hypothesis (e.g., "no website," "manual booking")

2. Source candidates:
   - Personal network (warm introductions best)
   - LinkedIn outreach (connect + message)
   - Industry Facebook groups (ask for interviews)
   - Local business associations
   - Cold email/SMS (lower response rate)

3. Target mix:
   - 3-4 "ideal" customers (perfect fit for hypothesis)
   - 2-3 "adjacent" customers (similar but different niche)
   - 1-2 "skeptics" (already using competitor solution)

**Outreach Template:**
```
Subject: Quick research interview - [Industry] operations

Hi [Name],

I'm researching how [industry] businesses handle [category] and would love
to learn from you. NOT selling anything - just trying to understand what's
working and what's broken.

Would you have 20 minutes this week for a quick call? I'll buy you coffee
next time I'm in [area] as a thank you.

Let me know a good time.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Tools:**
- LinkedIn (find candidates)
- Google Sheets (track candidates)
- Calendly (schedule interviews)

**Output:**
- List of 5-10 confirmed interview appointments

---

### Step 2: Prepare Interview Tracking Spreadsheet

**Objective:** Create system to track interview data for analysis

**Actions:**
1. Create Google Sheet: "Market Research - [Niche] - [Date]"
2. Add tabs:
   - **Candidates** (tracking who to interview)
   - **Responses** (raw interview notes)
   - **Analysis** (synthesized insights)

**Candidates Tab Columns:**
```
| Name | Business | Industry | Size (Revenue) | Contact | Status | Interview Date | Notes |
```

**Responses Tab Columns:**
```
| Interviewee | Date | Q1: Current Process | Q2: Recent Pain | Q3: Current Tools/Cost |
| Q4: Biggest Frustration | Q5: Cost of Inaction | Q6: Attempted Solutions |
| Q7: Recent Purchase | Q8: Budget Expectations | Q9: Decision Triggers |
| Q10: Commitment Test Result | Key Quotes | Follow-up Actions |
```

**Analysis Tab (filled out after 5+ interviews):**
```
| Pain Point | Frequency (%) | Severity (1-10) | Current Cost | Willingness to Pay | Priority |
```

**Tools:**
- Google Sheets
- Template: `templates/market-research-interview-tracker.xlsx`

**Output:**
- Tracking spreadsheet ready for data entry

---

### Step 3: Conduct Interviews

**Objective:** Execute interviews using script, take detailed notes

**Actions:**

1. **Before interview:**
   - Review their website/social media (5 min research)
   - Prepare questions (print script)
   - Set up recording (with permission)
   - Open tracking spreadsheet

2. **During interview:**
   - Ask for permission to record: "Mind if I record this? Just for my notes."
   - Follow script (10 questions)
   - Take notes in spreadsheet (Responses tab)
   - Listen more than talk (80/20 rule)
   - Capture exact quotes (use "..." in notes)
   - Note non-verbal cues (hesitation, excitement, frustration)

3. **After interview:**
   - Immediately fill out tracking spreadsheet (while fresh)
   - Highlight key quotes
   - Rate pain severity (your assessment)
   - Note commitment level (YES/MAYBE/NO for Q10)
   - Add follow-up actions (send prototype, schedule demo, etc.)

**Interview Tips:**
- ✅ DO: Ask "why" 3x to get to root cause
- ✅ DO: Stay silent after asking question (let them think)
- ✅ DO: Ask to see their tools/processes (screen share)
- ❌ DON'T: Pitch your solution (unless they ask after Q10)
- ❌ DON'T: Lead the witness ("This is a problem, right?")
- ❌ DON'T: Argue or defend your idea

**Tools:**
- Zoom (video call + recording)
- Google Sheets (live note-taking)
- Phone + voice memo app (backup recording)

**Output:**
- Completed interview notes in tracking spreadsheet (1 row per interview)

---

### Step 4: Analyze Interview Data (After 5+ Interviews)

**Objective:** Identify patterns, prioritize pain points, validate/invalidate hypotheses

**Actions:**

1. **Aggregate pain points:**
   - List all mentioned problems across interviews
   - Count frequency (how many people mentioned each?)
   - Rate average severity (1-10 based on their descriptions)
   - Estimate current cost (time/money lost per month)

2. **Fill out Analysis tab:**
   ```
   Example:
   | Pain Point | Frequency | Severity | Current Cost | WTP | Priority |
   |------------|-----------|----------|--------------|-----|----------|
   | Missed calls during rush | 7/10 (70%) | 8.5/10 | $8K/mo avg | $500-1K/mo | HIGH |
   | Manual booking process | 5/10 (50%) | 6/10 | 10 hrs/week | $100-300/mo | MEDIUM |
   | No online ordering | 9/10 (90%) | 9/10 | $12K/mo avg | $300-700/mo | HIGH |
   ```

3. **Identify patterns:**
   - What pain point was mentioned most frequently?
   - What pain point has highest severity + cost?
   - What pain point are people already paying to solve? (existing budget = strong signal)
   - What pain point got "YES" on commitment test (Q10)?

4. **Validate/Invalidate Hypotheses:**
   - **Validated:** If 60%+ mentioned pain + would pay to solve → BUILD THIS
   - **Invalidated:** If <40% mentioned or "nice to have" only → PIVOT
   - **Unclear:** If 40-60% mentioned → DO 5 MORE INTERVIEWS

**Tools:**
- Google Sheets (Analysis tab)
- Calculator (average severity, cost)

**Output:**
- Prioritized pain point list with validation status

---

### Step 5: Make Niche Decision

**Objective:** Decide which niche/pain point to pursue based on data

**Decision Matrix:**

| Factor | Weight | Score (1-10) | Weighted Score |
|--------|--------|--------------|----------------|
| **Pain Frequency** (how many people have this?) | 25% | [from analysis] | |
| **Pain Severity** (how painful is it?) | 25% | [from analysis] | |
| **Willingness to Pay** (do they have budget?) | 20% | [from Q8 responses] | |
| **Commitment Signal** (Q10 YES rate) | 15% | [% who said YES] | |
| **Market Size** (how many potential customers?) | 10% | [research-based] | |
| **Competition** (how crowded is this space?) | 5% | [10=low, 1=high] | |

**Scoring Example:**
```
Niche: Naples Restaurants - Voice AI for Missed Calls

| Factor | Weight | Score | Weighted |
|--------|--------|-------|----------|
| Frequency | 25% | 9 (90% mentioned) | 2.25 |
| Severity | 25% | 8.5 (very painful) | 2.13 |
| Willingness to Pay | 20% | 7 ($500-1K/mo budget) | 1.40 |
| Commitment | 15% | 8 (80% said YES to Q10) | 1.20 |
| Market Size | 10% | 6 (~500 restaurants in Naples) | 0.60 |
| Competition | 5% | 7 (few local competitors) | 0.35 |
| **TOTAL** | 100% | | **7.93/10** |

Decision Threshold:
- 8.0+ = STRONG GO (pursue immediately)
- 6.0-7.9 = CONDITIONAL GO (validate with small pilot)
- 4.0-5.9 = PIVOT (find different pain point in same niche)
- <4.0 = NO-GO (abandon niche)
```

**Actions:**
1. Score each validated pain point using matrix
2. Compare scores across different niches (if researching multiple)
3. Select highest-scoring niche/pain point
4. Document decision rationale

**Tools:**
- Google Sheets (create decision matrix tab)
- Calculator

**Output:**
```markdown
## Niche Decision: [Selected Niche + Pain Point]

**Score:** 7.93/10 (Conditional GO)

**Rationale:**
- 9/10 interviewees (90%) mentioned missing calls as top pain point
- Average lost revenue: $8,000/month per restaurant
- 8/10 (80%) said YES to being first customer if we built this
- Existing budget: Most already pay $200-500/mo for answering service (proves WTP)
- Market size: ~500 restaurants in Naples area (TAM: $4M/year if 50% adopt at $700/mo)

**Next Steps:**
1. Build MVP: Voice AI call answering for restaurants
2. Target first 5 customers from interviewees who said YES to Q10
3. Pilot pricing: $500/mo (below WTP ceiling, room to increase)
4. Success metric: 3/5 pilots convert to paying customers within 60 days
```

---

### Step 6: Share Findings & Get Feedback

**Objective:** Validate analysis with stakeholders before committing resources

**Actions:**
1. Create 1-page summary:
   - Niche selected + pain point
   - Key interview insights (top 3-5 quotes)
   - Decision matrix score
   - Recommended next steps

2. Share with:
   - Business partners (if any)
   - Trusted advisors/mentors
   - 1-2 interviewees (get their reaction to findings)

3. Incorporate feedback:
   - Did you miss anything?
   - Do findings resonate with their experience?
   - Any red flags you overlooked?

**Tools:**
- Google Doc (1-page summary)
- Email or Loom video (share findings)

**Output:**
- Validated niche decision with stakeholder feedback incorporated

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Interviewees won't open up | Asking hypotheticals or pitching too early | Use "Mom Test" questions, focus on past behavior, don't mention your idea until Q10 |
| Everyone says "great idea!" but won't commit | Being polite, not real pain | Ask Q10 commitment test earlier, look for hesitation, ask about past failed solutions |
| Interviewees cancel/no-show | Low investment in interview | Offer incentive (coffee, $25 gift card), warm intro vs cold outreach |
| Can't find people to interview | Wrong sourcing channels | Try Facebook groups, local meetups, referrals from first interviews |
| Interview data is conflicting | Interviewing wrong personas OR asking leading questions | Tighten persona definition, review question wording for bias |
| No clear pattern after 10 interviews | Niche too broad OR multiple distinct pain points | Segment interviews by sub-niche, analyze separately |

## Success Criteria

**Market research is complete when:**
- ✅ 5-10 interviews conducted with target personas
- ✅ All 10 questions answered per interview
- ✅ Tracking spreadsheet filled out completely
- ✅ Pain points prioritized by frequency + severity + WTP
- ✅ Niche decision made with score ≥6.0
- ✅ 3+ interviewees said YES to Q10 commitment test
- ✅ Next steps documented (MVP scope, first customers, pricing)

## Key Learnings Reference

**Strong Buy Signals:**
- "I'm already paying for [similar solution]" (existing budget)
- "This happened last week and cost us $X" (recent, quantified pain)
- "When can I see a demo?" (eagerness, urgency)
- "YES" to Q10 + asks for pricing (real commitment)

**Weak Buy Signals:**
- "This would be nice to have" (low priority)
- "I'd have to ask my boss/partner" (not decision maker)
- "Maybe in 6 months" (no urgency)
- "Great idea!" but deflects Q10 (polite rejection)

**Pivot Triggers:**
- <40% mention the pain point
- Pain is acknowledged but no one pays to solve it currently
- "I tried this before and it didn't work" (failed solutions in space)
- Q10 commitment test: <30% say YES
