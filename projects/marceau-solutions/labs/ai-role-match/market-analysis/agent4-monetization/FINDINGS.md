# Agent 4: Monetization Findings — AI Role-Match Hiring System

## Executive Summary
B2B HR SaaS has proven unit economics at scale, but customer acquisition costs are high ($450-$6,750 depending on segment), payback periods are long (10+ months), and the go-to-market for a solopreneur is the fundamental challenge. The best path is a usage-based or hybrid model targeting mid-market, but William's current constraints (full-time job starting April 6, no sales team, no enterprise relationships) make this a difficult monetization play.

## Recommended Pricing Model: Hybrid (Subscription + Usage)

### Why Hybrid?
- **Per-seat** (40-45% of B2B SaaS) is predictable but doesn't capture value from high-volume users
- **Usage-based** (30-35%) aligns cost with value but creates revenue unpredictability
- **Hybrid** (20% and growing) captures both: base subscription + per-screening/per-match fee

### Proposed Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Price | Includes | Target |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| **Starter** | $299/mo | 50 candidate screenings/mo, 3 active jobs, 2 users | SMB (10-50 employees) |
| **Growth** | $799/mo | 250 screenings/mo, 10 active jobs, 10 users, API access | Mid-market (50-250) |
| **Scale** | $1,999/mo | 1,000 screenings/mo, unlimited jobs, unlimited users, dedicated support | Mid-market+ (250-1000) |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Unlimited, custom integrations, SLA, compliance features | Enterprise (1000+) |
| **Overage** | $2/screening | Beyond tier limit | All tiers |

### Alternative: Per-Hire Success Fee
- Charge $50-$200 per successful hire (candidate accepted offer)
- Aligns perfectly with customer value ($4,700 avg cost per hire — saving even 10% = $470 value)
- Risk: tracking "successful hire" requires deep ATS integration

## Price Point the Market Would Bear

Based on competitive analysis:
- **SMB**: $200-$500/month (competing with Workable at $149/mo, Manatal at $15/user/mo)
- **Mid-market**: $500-$2,000/month (competing with Greenhouse at $1,000-$4,000/mo)
- **Sweet spot**: $500-$800/month for a focused AI matching layer that integrates with existing ATS
- **Key insight**: Don't try to replace the ATS — sell as an add-on/integration layer

## Customer Acquisition Costs (CAC)

| Segment | Estimated CAC | Source |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| SMB | $410 | Industry benchmark (Staffing & HR) |
| Mid-market | $1,912 | Industry benchmark |
| Enterprise | $6,754 | Industry benchmark |
| HR Tech average | $450-$612 | Multiple sources |

### CAC Channels
- **Content marketing/SEO**: Lowest CAC ($200-$400) but slowest (6-12 months to ramp)
- **LinkedIn ads**: $50-$200 cost per lead, ~$500-$1,500 CAC after conversion
- **G2/Capterra listings**: $300-$600 CAC, good for mid-market
- **Partnerships/integrations**: Near-zero CAC but requires engineering investment
- **Outbound sales**: $1,000-$3,000 CAC, requires a salesperson William doesn't have

## Lifetime Value (LTV) Estimates

| Tier | Monthly Revenue | Avg Retention | LTV |
|------|----------------|---------------|-----|
| Starter ($299/mo) | $299 | 12 months | $3,588 |
| Growth ($799/mo) | $799 | 18 months | $14,382 |
| Scale ($1,999/mo) | $1,999 | 24 months | $47,976 |

### LTV:CAC Ratios
- **Starter**: $3,588 / $410 = **8.8:1** (excellent if you can acquire at SMB cost)
- **Growth**: $14,382 / $1,912 = **7.5:1** (strong)
- **Scale**: $47,976 / $6,754 = **7.1:1** (healthy)
- **Industry benchmark**: 3.5:1 for HR Tech — these ratios look good on paper

### Reality Check
These LTV figures assume product-market fit and retention that hasn't been validated. The real challenge is getting to the first 10 paying customers, not optimizing unit economics.

## Break-Even Timeline

### Scenario A: Bootstrapped Solo (William's situation)
- **Fixed costs**: ~$500/month (API costs, hosting, basic tools)
- **Variable costs**: ~$0.10-$0.50 per screening (LLM API calls)
- **Revenue needed to break even on costs**: ~$500/month (2 Starter customers)
- **Revenue needed to be "worth it"**: $5,000+/month (time vs. opportunity cost of PT clients at $197/mo)
- **Timeline to $5K MRR**: 6-12 months with aggressive outreach (optimistic); 12-24 months more realistic
- **Key constraint**: William starts full-time job April 6. Who is doing sales, support, and product iteration?

### Scenario B: With Part-Time Sales Help
- **Additional cost**: $2,000-$5,000/month for a fractional SDR/AE
- **Break-even**: Higher bar ($7K-$10K/month) but faster customer acquisition
- **Timeline**: 3-6 months to $10K MRR (still optimistic)

## Adjacent Revenue Opportunities

1. **Talent analytics/insights reports** — "Here's what your ideal candidate looks like" (upsell)
2. **Workforce planning** — "Based on your current team composition, here are the gaps" (expansion)
3. **Candidate experience tool** — Let rejected candidates see their "ideal role" match (brand builder for employers)
4. **Recruiting agency white-label** — License the matching engine to agencies (partnership revenue)
5. **Job description optimization** — "Your JD is only 40% aligned with what you actually need" (adjacent feature)
6. **Compliance/bias audit** — Sell the transparency as a compliance product (timely given new regulations)

## Monetization Score: 2.5/5

**Rationale**: The unit economics look healthy on paper (7:1+ LTV:CAC ratios). The $500-800/month price point is viable for mid-market. However, the fundamental challenge is go-to-market: HR SaaS has high CAC ($450-$6,750), long sales cycles, and requires persistent sales effort. William's constraints (full-time job starting April 6, no sales team, no enterprise relationships, current focus on PT clients) make sustained customer acquisition extremely difficult. This is a product that requires a dedicated sales motion — it's not a product-led-growth or self-serve play. The adjacent revenue opportunities are real but compound the "need to build more" problem. Score reflects "viable economics, impractical execution for current situation."
