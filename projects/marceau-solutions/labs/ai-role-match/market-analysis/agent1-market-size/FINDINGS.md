# Agent 1: Market Size Findings — AI Role-Match Hiring System

## Executive Summary
The recruitment technology market is massive and growing. AI-specific recruitment tools are a fast-growing subset within a $169B+ talent acquisition ecosystem. However, the realistic obtainable market for a new entrant is modest given the heavy competition and enterprise sales cycles.

## TAM (Total Addressable Market)
- **Talent Acquisition & Staffing Technology Market**: $169 billion (2025), projected $308.4B by 2035 (6.2% CAGR)
- **Online Recruitment Technology Market**: $15.18B (2025), projected $46.07B by 2034 (12.9% CAGR)
- **Sources**: [Future Market Insights](https://www.futuremarketinsights.com/reports/talent-acquisition-and-staffing-technology-market), [Fortune Business Insights](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/online-recruitment-market-103730)

## SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
- **AI Recruitment Market (directly relevant)**: $707-752M (2025-2026), projected to $1.29B by 2035 (6.8-7.6% CAGR)
- **ATS Market**: $2.47-3.28B (2025), growing at 7.4-8.2% CAGR
- **Our SAM** (AI-powered candidate matching/ranking specifically): ~$500M-$750M globally
- **Sources**: [SkyQuest](https://www.skyquestt.com/report/ai-recruitment-market), [Technavio](https://www.technavio.com/report/ai-market-industry-in-recruitment-industry-analysis), [Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/ai-recruitment-market)

## SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market) — Year 1-3 Realistic
- **Year 1**: $50K-$200K (5-20 SMB/mid-market customers at $10K-$15K ACV)
- **Year 2**: $200K-$750K (15-50 customers with expansion revenue)
- **Year 3**: $500K-$2M (50-100+ customers, potentially enterprise deals)
- **Methodology**: Based on typical B2B SaaS ramp for bootstrapped/solopreneur operation selling into HR tech. Eightfold took years and $410M in funding to reach ~$97M ARR. As a bootstrapped operation, realistic SOM is much more conservative.

## Growth Rate
- **AI Recruitment**: 6.8-7.6% CAGR (solid but not explosive)
- **Online Recruitment Tech**: 12.9% CAGR (broader category, faster growth)
- **Overall Talent Acquisition Tech**: 6.2% CAGR
- **Key driver**: 87% of companies now use AI in hiring; 99% of Fortune 500 have it in their stack

## Key Market Trends

1. **Skills-based hiring replacing resume-first hiring**: 85% of employers now use skills-based hiring practices. Technical assessments are replacing resumes as primary signals. This SUPPORTS the product concept — matching on capabilities rather than keywords.

2. **AI adoption accelerating**: 52% of talent leaders planning to add AI agents to their teams in 2026. Over half of firms using AI screening saw KPIs improve 25%+.

3. **Massive funding flowing in**: $2.3B invested in HR/recruitment startups in first 9 months of 2025. Mercor ($350M), Turing ($111M), Ashby ($50M) — indicating strong investor confidence.

4. **Regulation tightening**: California AI hiring regulations effective Oct 2025, Colorado AI Act Feb 2026, NYC Local Law 144 active. This creates compliance complexity that could slow adoption OR create opportunity for compliant solutions.

5. **Consolidation underway**: Workday acquired Paradox (Oct 2025). Large players buying up niche tools. This means you either get acquired or get squeezed.

6. **Average cost per hire rising**: $4,700-$4,800 per hire (SHRM 2026), up 14% from 2019. Companies motivated to reduce this.

## Market Viability Score: 3.5/5

**Rationale**: The market is large ($750M SAM) and growing (7%+ CAGR), with clear demand signals. However, competition is intense with well-funded incumbents (Eightfold at $2.1B valuation, $410M raised). The realistic SOM for a bootstrapped solopreneur is modest. The growth rate is healthy but not the explosive 20%+ you'd want for a new market. Score reflects "big market, hard to capture."
