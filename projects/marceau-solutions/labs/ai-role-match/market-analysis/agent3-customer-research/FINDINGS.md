# Agent 3: Customer Research Findings — AI Role-Match Hiring System

## Executive Summary
The pain is real — HR teams spend 18+ hours per hire screening resumes, 27% report burnout, and 76% struggle to find qualified candidates. However, the buyer (HR Director / TA Lead) is a notoriously hard-to-reach enterprise buyer with long sales cycles. The skills-based hiring trend validates the product direction, but willingness to switch from existing ATS/AI tools is the key challenge.

## Target Customer Personas

### Primary: Mid-Market TA Lead (100-500 employees)
- **Title**: Director of Talent Acquisition, Head of Recruiting, VP People
- **Company size**: 100-500 employees, hiring 20-100 people/year
- **Current stack**: Greenhouse or Lever ATS + LinkedIn Recruiter + manual screening
- **Budget**: $12,000-$50,000/year for recruiting tools
- **Pain**: Drowning in applications, missing good candidates, spending too much time on screening
- **Decision process**: 2-4 week evaluation, needs buy-in from HR VP and possibly CTO

### Secondary: Recruiting Agency Owner
- **Title**: Agency Founder, Managing Partner
- **Size**: 5-50 recruiters placing for multiple clients
- **Current stack**: Bullhorn/JobAdder + LinkedIn + manual processes
- **Budget**: $5,000-$30,000/year for tools
- **Pain**: Need to place candidates faster than competitors; quality-of-match is their reputation
- **Decision process**: 1-2 weeks, founder makes the call

### Tertiary: Enterprise HR (500+ employees)
- **Title**: CHRO, SVP Talent
- **Budget**: $50,000-$500,000+ for recruiting tech
- **Pain**: Compliance, bias audits, volume screening
- **Decision process**: 3-12 months, procurement + legal + IT involved
- **Reality**: Almost impossible to land as a bootstrapped startup

## Pain Level: 7/10

### Evidence of Pain
- **18+ hours per hire** wasted on resume screening alone
- **27% of TA teams report burnout** from overwhelming volume
- **76% struggle to attract qualified candidates**, especially for specialized roles
- **60% of candidates abandon** lengthy/complex applications
- **$4,700-$4,800 average cost per hire** and rising
- **51% of organizations** still stuck in reactive hiring mode
- Over half of firms using AI screening saw **KPIs improve 25%+** — proving demand for AI solutions

### Why Not 9/10?
- Companies have workarounds (manual screening, outsourcing to agencies)
- Existing ATS tools partially solve the problem
- "Good enough" is the enemy — most companies tolerate inefficiency
- Switching costs are high (data migration, team retraining, process change)

## Current Solutions (How They Solve It Now)

| Solution | Market Share | Satisfaction | Cost |
|----------|-------------|-------------|------|
| **Manual screening** | Still dominant for SMBs | Low (18+ hrs/hire) | Time cost only |
| **ATS keyword filters** | 94% of large companies | Medium (misses good candidates) | $5K-$100K/yr |
| **AI add-ons** (HireVue, etc.) | Growing fast (87% use some AI) | Medium-High | $10K-$100K+/yr |
| **Staffing agencies** | ~$20B market | High quality but expensive | 15-25% of salary |
| **LinkedIn Recruiter** | Near-universal for sourcing | Medium | $10K+/yr |

## Willingness to Pay

- **SMB**: $200-$500/month ($2,400-$6,000/yr) — price-sensitive, need clear ROI
- **Mid-market**: $500-$2,000/month ($6,000-$24,000/yr) — will pay for proven results
- **Enterprise**: $3,000-$10,000+/month — but you can't reach them as a startup
- **Recruiting agencies**: $100-$300/recruiter/month — usage-based appeals here
- **Key insight**: At $4,700+ cost per hire, a tool that saves even 2-3 hours per hire or improves match quality by 15% pays for itself quickly

## Purchase Triggers

1. **High-volume hiring surge** — seasonal or growth-driven, suddenly need to screen 500+ applicants
2. **Bad hire aftermath** — expensive mis-hire ($15K-$240K cost) triggers search for better screening
3. **ATS contract renewal** — annual renewal is the moment they evaluate alternatives
4. **Compliance pressure** — new AI hiring laws (California, Colorado, NYC) force tool re-evaluation
5. **Recruiter burnout/turnover** — losing team members to overwork pushes automation search
6. **Executive mandate** — new CHRO/VP wants to modernize the stack

## Customer Research Score: 3.5/5

**Rationale**: The pain is real (7/10) and quantifiable ($4,700/hire, 18+ hrs screening). The market is actively adopting AI (87% use it). However, HR buyers are hard to reach, sales cycles are long (especially enterprise), switching costs are high, and the mid-market sweet spot still requires meaningful sales effort. A bootstrapped solopreneur faces a significant go-to-market challenge — this is fundamentally an enterprise sales motion, not a product-led growth play. Score reflects "real pain, hard-to-reach buyer."
