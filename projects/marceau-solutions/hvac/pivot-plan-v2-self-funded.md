## Strategic premises

1. **HVAC is now primary income, not secondary.** Every operational decision optimizes for revenue speed.
2. **W-2 applications continue in parallel.** Applying for I&E/SCADA roles takes ~2 hours/week and does not reduce HVAC time. A W-2 landing would be upside, not the plan.
3. **The dad-as-bridge structure is short** (~6 weeks). Goal is to compress the gap between "first paying customer" and "Marceau Solutions LLC contracts in its own name" as much as possible.
4. **Remote QA supervision after the training trip is documented and defensible.** Florida DBPR requires the Qualifying Agent to "primarily exercise management or supervisory functions" — this is achievable via documented video walkthroughs, recorded job review calls, signed-off quotes, and pulled-permit signatures. The plan builds in the supervision evidence trail from day one.
5. **Scope is tiered to William's skill ramp.** Start with Tier-1 service work (diagnostic, capacitor, contactor, refrigerant top-off, drain line) — the work that does not require senior judgment on site. Tier 2 and 3 unlock after demonstrated competence with documented video review.

## Dad's supervision structure (remote QA defensibility)

Florida DBPR enforcement against absentee QAs has historically targeted "license rental" arrangements with zero involvement. The defense against any future audit is **documented active supervision**. This plan creates that evidence trail:

| Supervision mechanism | Frequency | Evidence stored |
|---|---|---|
| Training trip (initial in-person) | 1× at launch (June) | Photos, video, signed training-completion checklist |
| Pre-job video walkthrough | Before any new job type | Video file in Google Drive, dated, indexed by customer + job # |
| Post-job close-out video | After every job | Same Drive folder |
| Weekly review Zoom | Every Sunday | Calendar invite, recorded call optional |
| Permit signatures | As required | Notarized signatures (FL permits typically allow mailed/scanned QA signatures for AC change-outs) |
| Quote approval | Every quote over $1,000 | Dad approves via email or signed text confirmation before quote goes to customer |
| Quarterly in-person visit (target) | 4× per year minimum | Travel logs |

**Backup plan if DBPR challenges supervision:** Documented evidence above + dad's flight records for in-person visits + recorded review calls. Florida case law (e.g., *Cardenas v. DBPR*) suggests QAs who maintain demonstrable supervision routines — even remotely — are defensible against "license front" findings.

**Job-scope discipline:** Until William earns his own Class A license (4-year experience requirement), no work is taken on that exceeds the supervision-via-video model. See Tiered Service Scope below.
