# Contractor's Final Payment Affidavit (Florida)

**Statutory source**: Florida Statute § 713.06(3)(d)(1) — Florida Construction Lien Law
**Purpose**: Prerequisite for a contractor to enforce a construction lien for final payment under FL law
**Saved**: 2026-05-26 (for Marceau Air HVAC operations reference)

---

## When to use this affidavit

This is a Florida Construction Lien Law document used by contractors to:
- Obtain final payment from a property Owner on a contracted construction job
- Establish the prerequisite for filing or enforcing a construction lien if payment is not made
- Document that all sub-tier lienors (subcontractors, suppliers, etc.) have been paid

**Applies to:**
- HVAC installation jobs with a written contract (residential install, commercial install)
- Significant repair or renovation work performed under contract
- Any job where the contractor may need to file a lien to collect final payment

**Does NOT typically apply to:**
- Routine residential service calls paid same-day (cash, check, card on completion)
- Small repair jobs without a written contract
- Time-and-materials work without lien rights asserted

**Critical legal requirement (per FS 713.06):**
- The contractor MUST execute this affidavit AND deliver it to the Owner at least **5 days before** instituting any action to enforce a lien
- Failure to give the Owner this affidavit means the contractor has NO lien or right of action against the Owner for labor, services, or materials under the direct contract — even if the contract is terminated for reasons other than completion
- This must be done regardless of whether the contractor has any sub-tier lienors

## Statutory form (verbatim from FS 713.06)

```

State of Florida
County of _________________

duly sworn, deposes and says of his or her personal knowledge
the following:

   _________________ (name of contractor's business), which

   (name of owner), hereinafter referred to as the "Owner," has
   furnished or caused to be furnished labor, materials, and

   contract.

   with section 713.06 of the Florida Statutes for the purposes
   of obtaining final payment from the Owner in the amount of
   $_________________.

   been paid in full, except the following listed lienors:

   NAME OF LIENOR          AMOUNT DUE
   _________________      $_________________
   _________________      $_________________
   _________________      $_________________

Signed, sealed, and delivered this _____ day of _____________,
_______.

By: _________________ (name of affiant)

    _________________ (title of affiant)

    _________________ (name of contractor's business)

```

## Statutory protections and risks (verbatim)

> The contractor shall have no lien or right of action against the owner for labor, services, or materials furnished under the direct contract while in default for not giving the owner the affidavit; however, the negligent inclusion or omission of any information in the affidavit which has not prejudiced the owner does not constitute a default that operates to defeat an otherwise valid lien. The contractor shall execute the affidavit and deliver it to the owner at least 5 days before instituting an action as a prerequisite to the institution of any action to enforce his or her lien under this chapter, even if the final payment has not become due because the contract is terminated for a reason other than completion and regardless of whether the contractor has any lienors working under him or her or not.

**Plain English translation:**

- If you (the contractor) don't deliver this affidavit to the Owner, you cannot file or enforce a lien for unpaid work. Full stop.
- Minor errors or omissions in the affidavit are forgivable IF they did not harm the Owner's interests.
- You must deliver the affidavit at least 5 days BEFORE starting any lien enforcement action.
- This requirement applies even if the contract was terminated early (not just completed jobs).
- This requirement applies whether you have sub-lienors or not.

## Practical workflow for Marceau Air

When this affidavit becomes relevant (NOT every job):

1. **Job qualifies if**: Written contract, install or substantial work, payment owed at completion
2. **At job completion**: Complete this affidavit with actual amounts
3. **Have Bill Sr. sign** in front of a notary in his location (Long Island NY or wherever)
4. **Notarized affidavit delivered to Owner** with the final invoice
5. **Maintain copy in job file** with photos, contracts, and signed acceptance
6. **If owner doesn't pay**: minimum 5-day wait after affidavit delivery before filing any lien-enforcement action

**For most residential service calls** (under ~$3,000, completed same-day, paid on completion): this affidavit is NOT needed. Get paid on the spot via Square/cash/check, give receipt, done.

**For installs or larger contract work** ($3,000+, multi-day, written contract): use this affidavit at completion as standard practice. Protects your lien rights even if you never need to enforce them.

## Related FL construction lien documents (for future reference)

Future templates to add to this folder when needed:

- **Notice to Owner** (NTO) — required by sub-tier lienors within 45 days of first labor/material delivery; FS 713.06(2)(a)
- **Notice of Commencement** (NOC) — filed by the owner before work starts on jobs over $5,000; FS 713.13. As contractor, you should verify owner has filed this.
- **Waiver and Release of Lien upon Final Payment** — sub-tier lienor release form; FS 713.20(2)
- **Conditional Waiver and Release upon Progress Payment** — for progress payments; FS 713.20(2)

These come into play when work scales up (larger installs, commercial work, sub-tier relationships). For initial Marceau Air residential service operations, you likely won't need them. Save reference templates as the business matures.
