## EPA Section 608 Certification (William)

**Required by EPA**: Anyone who handles refrigerants on stationary AC systems must be certified. Without this, you legally cannot touch refrigerant — and most service calls involve refrigerant.

**Certification types:**
- **Type I**: Small appliances (5 lb or less of refrigerant) — window units, dehumidifiers
- **Type II**: High-pressure systems (residential split systems, most commercial) — MOST CRITICAL for HVAC service
- **Type III**: Low-pressure systems (commercial chillers) — not needed for residential service
- **Universal**: All three types (highest value, recommended)

**Recommendation**: Take **Universal** certification — costs about the same as Type II alone, future-proofs you for any work.

**Providers:**

| Provider | URL | Cost (Universal) | Format | Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ESCO Institute** | escogroup.org | $25-$50 (online proctored) | Online study + proctored exam | 1-2 days study + 2-hr exam |
| **Mainstream Engineering** | epatest.com | $20-$50 | Online or mail-in | 1-2 days study + exam |
| **HVAC Excellence** | hvacexcellence.org | $30-$60 | Online | 1-2 days |
| **1-800-EPA-TEST** | epa608.com | $25-$50 | Online | 1-2 days |

**Action plan for William's EPA 608:**
1. Buy study guide ($10-20) or use free online materials
2. Study 8-15 hours (the test is real but passable with effort)
3. Take online proctored exam through ESCO or Mainstream
4. Print certification card — carry it on jobs (legally required)
