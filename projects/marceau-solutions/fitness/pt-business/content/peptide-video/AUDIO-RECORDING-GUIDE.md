# Peptide Video - Audio Recording Guide

**Method:** Audio-First (record audio, then video with lip-sync)
**Total Read Time:** ~10-12 minutes
**Tone:** Like explaining to a friend at the gym

---

## Recording Setup

1. **Quiet room** - no AC, fans, or background noise
2. **Phone/mic close** - 6-12 inches from mouth
3. **Water nearby** - stay hydrated
4. **Stand or sit comfortably** - standing gives more energy
5. **Read through once silently** before recording

## SECTION 1: What Are Peptides?

Okay, first things first - let's make sure we're all on the same page about what a peptide actually is. Because I see a lot of confusion out there.

[BREATH]

So basically, a peptide is just a chain of amino acids. You know amino acids - they're the building blocks of protein. When you string together less than fifty of them, that's a peptide. More than fifty... and now you're talking about a protein.

[BREATH]

Here's what's cool though - your body **already** makes thousands of these things naturally. They're like little messengers that tell your cells what to do.

Think about insulin - that's a peptide. Growth hormone? Your body releases that because of peptides signaling it to.

[BREATH]

Now, the peptides that everyone in fitness is hyped about - these are **synthetic** versions of those natural signaling molecules. Scientists designed them to trigger specific things in your body.

Like, you want more growth hormone release? There's a peptide for that. Faster tissue repair? Yep. Less inflammation? That too.

[BREATH]

But here's where most people get it wrong... and this is important.

---

## SECTION 2: Categories of Peptides

Alright, so peptides aren't just one thing. They fall into a bunch of different categories... and understanding this is gonna help you cut through a lot of the noise out there.

Let me walk you through the main ones you're gonna hear about.

[BREATH]

**First up - Growth Hormone Secretagogues.**

I know, it's a mouthful. But basically these are peptides that tell your pituitary gland to pump out more of your **own** growth hormone.

And look, the research on some of these goes back to the eighties - this isn't brand new stuff. But here's the catch - most of them are **not** FDA approved for the reasons people in fitness are using them.

[BREATH]

Let me give you some examples.

Sermorelin - this one was actually FDA approved from nineteen ninety-seven to two thousand eight. Good safety track record.

CJC-twelve ninety-five - extends how long your growth hormone stays elevated. Still a research compound though.

Ipamorelin - this one mimics ghrelin and triggers a GH pulse. People like it because it's pretty selective.

And then there's Tesamorelin - and this is the **only** one that's currently FDA approved... but it's for HIV-related lipodystrophy, not for fitness.

[BREATH]

**Second category - Healing and Recovery Peptides.**

Okay, this is honestly what got me interested in all this in the first place.

BPC-one fifty-seven - stands for Body Protection Compound. There's research on it for gut health and tissue repair. People swear by this stuff.

TB-five hundred - this is a fragment of something called Thymosin Beta-4. Studied for wound healing.

[BREATH]

**Third - Performance and Body Composition.**

GHRP-six - another growth hormone releaser.

MK-six seventy-seven - now technically this isn't even a peptide, but everyone groups it here so I'm including it.

AOD-ninety-six oh-four - this is actually a fragment of growth hormone itself, and it's been studied for fat metabolism.

[BREATH]

**And finally - Cognitive and Longevity peptides.**

Epithalon - there's research on telomere effects with this one.

Semax - studied for cognitive function.

Selank - studied for anti-anxiety effects.

[BREATH]

Now I wanna be really clear about something. **Most** of these do **not** have FDA approval for the stuff people are using them for.

The research exists, sure - but a lot of it is animal studies or really small human trials. That's just the reality of where we're at.

## SECTION 3: The Science vs. The Hype

Okay, this is where I gotta keep it real with you.

[BREATH]

The fitness industry has this tendency to take early-stage research - like, one study on mice - and blow it **completely** out of proportion.

Remember BPC-one fifty-seven from the healing category? You'll see people claiming it heals literally **everything**. Or that some peptide is gonna completely transform your physique overnight.

[BREATH]

So now that you know the different categories, let me show you where each of these actually falls on the evidence spectrum.

[BREATH]

**What actually has solid research behind it:**

There's a handful of peptides that have real, legit research behind them.

Tesamorelin - remember, I mentioned this is the only one that's currently FDA approved. Real clinical trials, the whole nine yards. Now it's approved for HIV-related lipodystrophy, not fitness... but the point is, it went through the full process.

[BREATH]

Sermorelin - this one I mentioned earlier too. It was FDA approved from ninety-seven to two thousand eight - they only stopped making it because of manufacturing stuff, not because it was dangerous. That's a solid safety track record.

[BREATH]

And those GHRP compounds I talked about - GHRP-six and GHRP-two? Those have like thirty plus years of research behind them. Now most of that is animal studies and smaller human trials, but there's a real body of evidence there.

[BREATH]

**What has promising but limited research:**

Now here's where a lot of the exciting stuff falls - but also where you gotta be careful.

BPC-one fifty-seven and TB-five hundred - both from that healing category - a lot of the studies are on rodents. There's some human case reports, but we don't have the big clinical trials yet. Promising? Yeah. Proven? Not quite.

[BREATH]

**And then there's the stuff that's mostly just hype:**

Red flags to watch for:

Anything sold as a "miracle" - run the other way.

Extreme fat loss claims - be skeptical. Remember AOD-ninety-six oh-four from the body comp category? Even that one, which is literally a fragment of growth hormone - the research is mixed.

"No side effects whatsoever" - that's basically **never** true for anything that actually does something in your body.

[BREATH]

Here's the honest truth. A lot of these peptides probably **do** work to some degree. The early research is genuinely interesting.

But we don't have the long-term human data that we'd ideally want. So when you hear about Tesamorelin or Sermorelin - those have real track records. When you hear about some of the newer stuff - just understand where it falls on that spectrum.

So the question you're probably asking is - should **I** actually look into peptides?

You haven't actually nailed down the fundamentals yet. Peptides aren't gonna fix a bad diet or inconsistent training.

At the end of the day, peptides are **tools**. Like any tool, they can be useful in the right situation when you know what you're doing.

But they're not magic... and they come with real unknowns.

Go in with your eyes open.

# RECORDING WORKFLOW

## Step 1: Audio Recording (Today)

1. Find quiet space
2. Open this guide on phone/tablet
3. Record full script in ONE take (or section by section)
4. **Don't stop for mistakes** - just pause, restart the sentence
5. Save audio file as `peptide_audio_master.m4a`

## Step 2: Video Recording (After Audio)

1. Play audio in **one earbud** (leave other ear open)
2. Set up camera (rear camera, good lighting)
3. Lip-sync along with audio - doesn't need to be perfect
4. Record each section as separate video file
5. B-roll will cover any sync issues

## Step 3: Editing

1. Sync audio to video in timeline
2. Cut talking head where B-roll goes
3. Add B-roll clips over voice
4. Add text overlays
5. Export
