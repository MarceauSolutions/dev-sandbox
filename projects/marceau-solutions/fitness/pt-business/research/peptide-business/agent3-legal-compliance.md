# Agent 3: Legal & Compliance Framework

## Executive Summary

**The Core Problem:** A personal trainer CANNOT legally recommend peptides to clients in Florida without risking felony charges (practicing medicine without license).

**The Only Compliant Model:** Pure education separated from client supplementation, with referrals to licensed medical providers.

## Florida Law: What Crosses the Line

**ILLEGAL (Practicing Medicine Without License):**
- "You should take BPC-157 for your gut"
- "This peptide will help your muscle recovery"
- Recommending peptides for specific conditions
- Interpreting blood work to justify peptide use
- Providing dosing guidance
- Facilitating peptide access

**LEGAL:**
- "Peptides are amino acid chains being researched..."
- "Here's educational information about peptide science"
- "I can't recommend, but here are medical professionals who can"
- General fitness and nutrition coaching

**Penalties:**
- Up to 5 years prison
- Up to $5,000 fine
- Professional reputation destroyed

## Required Disclaimers

```
PEPTIDE EDUCATION DISCLAIMER

I am a personal trainer/fitness coach, NOT a physician or medical professional.
Any information about peptides is educational only, NOT a recommendation.
I do not prescribe, recommend, diagnose, or treat with peptides.
Any decisions regarding peptide use are entirely your own responsibility.
Consult appropriate medical professionals before using any substances.
```

## Insurance Reality

**Standard PT insurance likely does NOT cover peptide-related claims because:**
- Peptides are unapproved drugs
- Outside standard scope of practice
- High liability territory

**Before offering peptide services:** Get written confirmation from insurance that you're covered.

## The Only Compliant Business Model

**SAFE: Education + Referral Model**
1. Educate about peptide SCIENCE (not recommendations)
2. Do NOT recommend specific peptides to clients
3. Refer interested clients to licensed providers
4. Keep fitness coaching and peptide education SEPARATE
5. No product sales or commissions

**NOT SAFE:**
- Recommending peptides
- Facilitating access
- Dosing guidance
- Monitoring results
- Profiting from peptide usage
