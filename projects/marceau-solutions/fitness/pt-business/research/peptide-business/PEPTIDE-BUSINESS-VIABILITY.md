## The Core Question

> "How do I legally connect clients with peptide access while running a PT business in Naples, FL?"

---

## Executive Summary

**The Hard Truth:** The peptides you want to help clients with (BPC-157, TB-500) are **illegal to prescribe** as of 2024. The FDA banned compounding pharmacies from making them.

**The Legal Reality:**
- Recommending peptides to clients = practicing medicine without a license = **felony in Florida**
- "Research Use Only" disclaimers don't protect you if context suggests human use
- Standard fitness insurance won't cover peptide-related claims

**The Viable Path Forward:**
1. **Education-Only Model** - Teach peptide science, never recommend
2. **Refer to Telemedicine Clinics** - For FDA-approved peptides (Tesamorelin, Sermorelin, Semaglutide)
3. **Get Certified** - Empowerd Academy teaches the legal "RUO model"
4. **Partner with Local Clinic** - Non-financial referral arrangement (financial = felony in FL)

## The Three Viable Business Models

### Model 1: Education + Referral (RECOMMENDED)

**How it works:**
1. You educate clients about peptide science (what they are, how they work)
2. You do NOT recommend specific peptides
3. When clients ask "should I take X?" → "I can't recommend, but here are providers who can help"
4. You refer to telemedicine clinics (Marek Health, Regenics) or local Naples clinics
5. Client gets prescription from licensed provider
6. You continue fitness coaching optimized for their protocol

**Revenue:**
- Fitness coaching fees (your main revenue)
- Possible affiliate commissions from telemedicine clinics (Marek Health has program)
- Premium pricing for "peptide-optimized" training packages

**Legal Safety:** HIGH (if you stay in education lane)

---

### Model 2: Local Clinic Partnership (VIABLE BUT CAREFUL)

**How it works:**
1. Partner with Naples clinic (T Clinics USA, Atlantic Men's Clinic, Liquivida)
2. **NON-FINANCIAL arrangement** - you refer, they refer back
3. Educational collaboration (workshops, content together)
4. Client sees them for peptides, sees you for fitness

**Critical Warning:** Florida Patient Brokering Act makes paid referrals a FELONY
- No referral fees
- No revenue share
- No commission per client

**Revenue:**
- Fitness coaching from referred clients
- Workshop fees
- Premium "clinic partnership" packages

**Legal Safety:** MEDIUM (must avoid ANY financial exchange for referrals)

**Next Step:** Consult Florida healthcare attorney ($1,000-2,000) before formalizing

---

### Model 3: White-Label Telemedicine (ADVANCED)

**How it works:**
1. Partner with platform like Ola Digital Health
2. Build your own branded telehealth service
3. Licensed providers (their network) prescribe
4. You handle marketing + fitness coaching

**Example:** Florida trainer enrolled 60 clients in 3 months, doubled income

**Revenue:**
- Higher margins than referral model
- Own the client relationship
- Recurring revenue from subscriptions

**Legal Safety:** HIGH (licensed providers handle prescriptions)

**Investment:** Higher startup cost, more complex

## GO / NO-GO Decision Framework

### GO Signals (Proceed with peptide-focused business)

- [ ] Accept education-only model (no recommendations)
- [ ] Willing to invest $3,000-5,500 in certifications + legal review
- [ ] Comfortable referring to licensed providers (not facilitating gray market)
- [ ] Understand FDA-approved peptides are the legal foundation
- [ ] Accept that BPC-157/TB-500 can't be part of legitimate business model

### PAUSE Signals (Reconsider approach)

- [ ] Want to recommend specific peptides to clients
- [ ] Want to profit from gray market peptide sales
- [ ] Not willing to invest in proper certifications
- [ ] Uncomfortable with referral model

### NO-GO Signals (Don't pursue this)

- [ ] Planning to sell or facilitate access to research peptides
- [ ] Unwilling to stay in education lane
- [ ] Want to provide dosing/protocol guidance
- [ ] Expecting to build business on banned peptides
