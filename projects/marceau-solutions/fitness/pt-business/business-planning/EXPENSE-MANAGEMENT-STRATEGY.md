## Credit Card Strategy: Two AmEx Business Cash Cards

### Card Assignments

| Card | Label | Use For | Why |
|------|-------|---------|-----|
| **AmEx Business Cash #1** | **Operations Card** | Recurring subscriptions, monthly services, software | Predictable monthly charges — easy to forecast burn rate |
| **AmEx Business Cash #2** | **Growth Card** | Equipment, one-time purchases, ad spend, investments | Variable spend — easy to track growth investments vs. operating costs |

### Operations Card (Card #1) — Recurring / Fixed Costs

| Category | Examples | Frequency |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Business Coaching | Propane Fitness (split payment) | Monthly |
| Software/SaaS | Hosting, API subscriptions, Skool ($9/mo) | Monthly |
| Communication | Twilio SMS (~$12/mo) | Monthly |
| Insurance | Business liability | Monthly |
| Domains | marceausolutions.com renewal | Annual |
| Streaming/Tools | Any recurring tool subscriptions | Monthly |

### Growth Card (Card #2) — Variable / Investment Costs

| Category | Examples | Frequency |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Equipment | Teleprompter, microphones, camera gear, lighting | As needed |
| Advertising | Meta ads ($15-20/day when running) | Variable |
| Education | Courses, certifications, books | As needed |
| Content Production | Props, wardrobe, gym memberships for filming | As needed |
| Business Development | Networking events, local partnerships, business cards | As needed |
| One-Time Software | Lifetime deals, template purchases | As needed |

### Why This Split Works

1. **Cash back optimization** — Each AmEx Blue Business Cash earns 2% on the first $50K/year. Two cards = $100K at 2% before dropping to 1%. At your current burn rate (~$5K/mo), one card covers it, but as ad spend scales, you'll need the headroom.

2. **Credit utilization** — Spreading spend across two cards keeps each card's utilization ratio lower, which helps business credit score.

3. **Instant categorization** — Statement from Card #1 = cost of doing business. Statement from Card #2 = growth investments. No manual tagging needed.

4. **Tax prep** — Equipment on Card #2 may qualify for Section 179 deduction or de minimis safe harbor (under $2,500). Having them on a separate card makes your accountant's job trivial.

5. **Budget enforcement** — If Card #2 spending is climbing but revenue isn't, that's a clear signal to pause growth investments.

### The Propane Fitness Split

You're already splitting Propane payments across both cards. This is fine — Propane is both an operating cost (ongoing coaching) AND a growth investment (building your business). The split keeps utilization balanced. Just note in your records which portion went to which card.

## Expense Categories (Tax-Ready)

These categories align with Schedule C (Self-Employment) deductions:

| Category | Tax Line | Card | Examples |
|----------|----------|------|----------|
| **Advertising** | Line 8 | Growth | Meta ads, Google ads, boosted posts |
| **Business Insurance** | Line 15 | Ops | Liability insurance |
| **Office Supplies** | Line 18 | Growth | Teleprompter, mics, ring light, SD cards |
| **Software/SaaS** | Line 18 (Other) | Ops | Hosting, APIs, Skool, tool subscriptions |
| **Communication** | Line 25 (Utilities) | Ops | Twilio, phone line costs |
| **Education** | Line 27a (Other) | Growth | Propane Fitness, courses, certifications |
| **Contract Labor** | Line 11 | Growth | Freelancers, VA hire (future) |
| **Professional Services** | Line 17 | Ops | Accountant, legal (when needed) |
| **Equipment (>$2,500)** | Form 4562 | Growth | Major gear (depreciated over useful life) |
| **Equipment (<$2,500)** | Line 18 | Growth | Small gear (de minimis safe harbor, expense immediately) |

### De Minimis Safe Harbor Rule

Equipment under **$2,500 per item** can be expensed immediately (deducted in full the year purchased) instead of depreciated over multiple years. This likely covers:
- Teleprompter (~$100-300)
- Microphones (~$50-400 each)
- Ring lights (~$30-100)
- Tripods, mounts, accessories

If a single item exceeds $2,500 (unlikely for your current needs), it must be depreciated under Section 179 or MACRS.

## Monthly Expense Tracking Process

### What to Track

For every business expense, record:
1. **Date** of purchase
2. **Vendor** (who you paid)
3. **Amount** (pre-tax)
4. **Category** (from table above)
5. **Card used** (Ops or Growth)
6. **Description** (what it's for — one line)
7. **Receipt** (photo/PDF saved)

### Where to Track

| Tool | What It Handles | Who Uses It |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| **Fitness Influencer Platform** (web UI) | Daily expense logging, category assignment, monthly summaries | You (as end user testing the platform) |
| **Google Sheets** (Billing tab) | Revenue tracking (already exists), cross-reference with expenses | Automated via n8n |
| **Claude Code / 3-Agent System** | Advanced analytics, tax prep, quarterly projections, budget vs. actual analysis | You (competitive edge tools) |
| **Propane Fitness Framework** | Business coaching metrics, KPIs they require you to track | Follow their process as prescribed |

### Monthly Cadence

| When | What | Where |
|------|------|-------|
| **Daily** | Log any business purchase | Fitness Influencer platform (task or expense log) |
| **Weekly (Sunday)** | Review week's expenses, ensure nothing missed | Platform dashboard |
| **Monthly (1st)** | Reconcile both card statements against logged expenses | Claude Code (advanced analysis) |
| **Monthly (1st)** | Calculate: total ops cost, total growth cost, revenue, profit/loss | Platform summary + Claude Code deep analysis |
| **Quarterly** | Tax estimate review, adjust projected quarterly payments | Claude Code / accountant |

## The Two-Track Approach: Platform User vs. Power User

### Track 1: Fitness Influencer Platform (End-User Perspective)

**Goal**: Use the platform the way a paying customer would. Find friction, bugs, and missing features.

Use the platform's web UI for:
- Logging daily business tasks (task manager with XP)
- Tracking content creation (video tools, scheduling)
- Managing client interactions (when clients arrive)
- Propane program task tracking (daily missions)
- **NEW: Expense logging** (feature to be built — identified gap)

**Why this matters**: When you sell these systems, the end user is a fitness coach who doesn't know what VS Code is. If you can't run your business through the web UI, neither can they. Every friction point you hit is a product improvement opportunity.

### Track 2: Claude Code + 3-Agent Architecture (Competitive Edge)

**Goal**: Use advanced tools that your competitors and customers won't have.

Use Claude Code / Clawdbot / Ralph for:
- Deep financial analysis and projections
- Automated content generation at scale
- Complex n8n workflow debugging and optimization
- Codebase improvements to the platform itself
- Multi-agent market research and strategy
- Tax preparation and quarterly estimate calculations
- Advanced analytics your platform doesn't surface yet

**Why this matters**: Your edge isn't just that you built the platform — it's that you use tools on top of it that nobody else has. The platform is what you sell. The 3-agent system is what makes YOU faster than everyone else.

### Track 3: Propane Fitness (The Program)

**Goal**: Go through their program exactly as designed. Extract what works. Discard what doesn't.

Follow Propane's framework for:
- The 12-week business coaching curriculum
- Their prescribed KPIs and tracking metrics
- Their content strategy recommendations
- Their client acquisition playbook
- Their accountability system

**What to take**: Proven frameworks, templates, accountability structures, marketing psychology.

**What to leave**: Anything that duplicates what your tech already does better. If Propane says "use this CRM" but your platform + Google Sheets handles it, note the gap and move on.

**Critical rule**: Don't skip steps in their program. You paid $8,530 for structured guidance. Play the game, complete every assignment, THEN evaluate what was useful. Skipping ahead means you miss context that might matter later.
