**Meeting Date:** 2026-02-05
**Purpose:** Explore business loan options for peptide-focused personal training business
**Outcome Goal:** Get specific terms, understand requirements, make informed decision

---

## Meeting Objectives

1. Understand exact loan terms and costs
2. Determine qualification requirements
3. Assess risk to personal finances

5. Get timeline for funding availability

---

## Questions to Ask (Prioritized)

### CRITICAL - Ask These First

| # | Question | Why It Matters | Notes |
|---|----------|---------------|-------|
| 1 | **What are the actual interest rates and APR?** | Total cost of borrowing | Get the APR, not just "low rates" |
| 2 | **Is this a personal loan or business loan?** | Affects liability and credit | Business loans may require LLC |
| 3 | **What are the qualification requirements?** | Know if you even qualify | Credit score, revenue history, collateral |
| 4 | **What happens if the business fails?** | Understand personal risk | Are you personally liable? |
| 5 | **Are there prepayment penalties?** | Can you pay off early without fees? | Important if business succeeds fast |

### IMPORTANT - Ask Second

| # | Question | Why It Matters | Notes |
|---|----------|---------------|-------|
| 6 | **What's the repayment schedule?** | Monthly cash flow impact | Daily, weekly, monthly? |
| 7 | **How quickly can funds be available?** | Planning timeline | Same day? 1 week? |
| 8 | **What documentation do I need?** | Preparation requirements | Tax returns, bank statements, business plan? |
| 9 | **Is collateral required?** | What's at risk? | Personal assets? Equipment? |
| 10 | **Does this affect my personal credit score?** | Long-term impact | Hard inquiry, reporting |

### GOOD TO KNOW

| # | Question | Why It Matters | Notes |
|---|----------|---------------|-------|
| 11 | **What's your revenue model?** | Understand their incentives | Origination fees? Factor rates? |
| 12 | **Can I use funds for any business purpose?** | Flexibility of spending | Some lenders restrict use |
| 13 | **Do you report to business credit bureaus?** | Building business credit | Could help or hurt |
| 14 | **What's your typical client profile?** | Are you the right fit? | Do they work with startups? |
| 15 | **Are there any hidden fees?** | Total cost transparency | Origination, processing, late fees |

## Red Flags to Watch For

| Red Flag | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| **Won't give you APR in writing** | Hiding true cost |
| **Pressure to sign today** | High-pressure sales tactic |
| **Daily repayment required** | Cash flow killer for startups |
| **Factor rate instead of interest rate** | Often much more expensive than it sounds |
| **Personal guarantee required on business loan** | You're personally liable anyway |
| **Vague about qualification requirements** | May change terms after you're invested |
| **Extremely fast approval with no documentation** | Likely very high rates |

## Decision Framework After the Call

### Take the Loan IF:

- [ ] APR is under 20%
- [ ] No daily repayment requirement
- [ ] Prepayment allowed without penalty
- [ ] Clear about personal liability
- [ ] Total repayment cost is acceptable
- [ ] You've validated client demand first (see viability testing)

### Walk Away IF:

- [ ] APR over 30%
- [ ] Factor rate makes true cost unclear
- [ ] Pressure to sign immediately
- [ ] Terms change from initial conversation
- [ ] Daily/weekly repayment required
- [ ] Requires collateral you can't afford to lose

### Negotiate IF:

- APR is 20-30% (try to get lower)
- Terms are almost acceptable but not quite
- They seem flexible

---

## Key Principle

**Don't take on debt until you've validated demand.**

Before signing any loan:
1. Get 3-5 paying clients (even at reduced rate)
2. Confirm you can close leads
3. Test your marketing message
4. Know your customer acquisition cost

If you can't get 5 clients with $0 marketing budget (word of mouth, social, networking), a loan won't fix that.
