# Workflow: Legal Research

## Overview
Research case law, statutes, regulations, and precedents relevant to the housing discrimination claim. Organize findings for attorney review and case strategy.

## When to Use
- Preparing a filing or response
- Evaluating legal strategy options
- Understanding specific legal concepts or defenses
- Preparing for hearing or mediation

## Key Research Sources

### Federal Statutes
- **Fair Housing Act**: 42 USC 3601-3619 (full text at uscode.house.gov)
- **Civil Rights Act of 1866**: 42 USC 1982 (race discrimination in property)
- **ADA/Section 504**: If disability discrimination involved

### Florida Statutes
- **FL Fair Housing Act**: Chapter 760 (statutes.online.com)
- **FL Landlord-Tenant Law**: Chapter 83
- **FL Human Rights Act**: Chapter 760.01-760.11

### Case Law Research
- **Google Scholar** (scholar.google.com) - Free case law search
- **CourtListener** (courtlistener.com) - Free federal case search
- **Justia** (justia.com) - Free case summaries
- **Casetext** (casetext.com) - AI-powered legal research (paid)

### Agency Resources
- **HUD Fair Housing**: hud.gov/program_offices/fair_housing_equal_opp
- **FCHR**: fchr.myflorida.com
- **DOJ Civil Rights**: justice.gov/crt/housing-and-civil-enforcement-section

### Local Resources
- Florida Bar Lawyer Referral Service: 1-800-342-8011
- Legal Aid Service of Collier County (if applicable)
- Local fair housing organizations

## Steps

### 1. Define Research Question
Before searching, write the specific legal question:
- "Can a landlord [specific action] under the Fair Housing Act?"
- "What constitutes [specific type of] discrimination under FL Chapter 760?"
- "What damages are available for [specific claim]?"

### 2. Search for Relevant Law
Start broad, then narrow:
1. Federal statute (FHA) → specific section
2. Florida statute → specific section
3. Case law interpreting that section
4. Cases in your jurisdiction (11th Circuit, Middle/Southern District of FL)

### 3. Document Findings
For each relevant case or statute, note:
- **Citation**: Full legal citation
- **Holding**: What the court decided
- **Relevance**: How it applies to your case
- **Favorable or Unfavorable**: Does it help or hurt?
- **Distinguishable**: If unfavorable, how is your case different?

### 4. Organize by Topic
Group research findings by legal issue:
- Elements of discrimination claim
- Types of evidence sufficient to prove discrimination
- Available remedies and damages
- Defenses the respondent may raise
- Procedural requirements

### 5. Prepare Research Memo
For attorney review or self-representation:
```markdown
## Legal Research: [Topic]
**Date**: [YYYY-MM-DD]
**Question**: [Specific legal question]

### Applicable Law
- [Statute 1]: [Summary]
- [Statute 2]: [Summary]

### Key Cases
- [Case 1 citation]: [Holding and relevance]
- [Case 2 citation]: [Holding and relevance]

### Analysis
[How the law applies to your specific facts]

### Conclusion
[Answer to the research question]
```

## Common Legal Issues in Housing Discrimination

| Issue | Key Law | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Disparate treatment | 42 USC 3604(a) | Intent to discriminate |
| Disparate impact | *Inclusive Communities* (2015) | No intent needed, just effect |
| Retaliation | 42 USC 3617 | For exercising fair housing rights |
| Reasonable accommodation | 42 USC 3604(f)(3)(B) | Disability context |
| Steering | 42 USC 3604(a) | Directing to/away from areas |
| Discriminatory terms | 42 USC 3604(b) | Different conditions based on protected class |

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Can't find relevant case law | Broaden search terms, try different databases, consult attorney |
| Conflicting case law | Note the conflict, identify which court's decisions control |
| Law seems unfavorable | Research exceptions, distinguishing factors, or alternative theories |
