## Key Findings Summary

### Positive Signals

1. **No MCP Competitors**: First-mover advantage in AI ticket aggregation space
2. **Massive TAM**: $50-85B ticketing market with 8-11% CAGR
3. **AI Agent Explosion**: 44.8% CAGR, MCP is now industry standard
4. **First Transactions Happening**: Mastercard + VOX Cinemas (Nov 2025), Google AI Mode
5. **High Margins**: 95%+ gross margin on API/transaction fees
6. **Multiple Revenue Paths**: Subscription, transaction fees, affiliate fallback

### Red Flags

1. **BOTS Act**: Automated ticket purchasing is explicitly illegal ($31M+ in enforcement actions)
2. **Platform ToS**: All major platforms prohibit bots, scraping, automated purchasing
3. **API Access Barriers**: Partner APIs require existing business relationships
4. **Anti-Bot Systems**: Ticketmaster blocks 5 billion bot attempts/month
5. **Long Path to Revenue**: 4-5 years to $1M ARR
6. **Platform Dependency**: APIs could be revoked at any time

## Opportunities Identified

### Blue Ocean: B2B Pivot

Instead of consumer AI agents, target:
- **Enterprise Perks Platforms** (TicketsatWork has 40,000+ corporate clients)
- **Licensed Ticket Brokers** (~2,500 in NY alone)
- **Travel/Concierge Services** (already have legal frameworks)

### Phased Approach

1. **Phase 1 (MVP)**: Search-only MCP using public Discovery APIs
2. **Phase 2 (Monetization)**: Add affiliate integrations (earn commission)
3. **Phase 3 (Full Integration)**: Pursue official partnerships over time
