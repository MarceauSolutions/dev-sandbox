# Agent 1: Pure Value-Based Pricing Analysis

**Evaluation Date:** 2026-01-17
**Model:** Pure Value-Based Pricing (Nick Saraev Framework)
**Business Context:** Marceau Solutions - AI Automation Agency

---

## Executive Summary

Pure value-based pricing offers the highest profit margins when executed correctly but requires significant sales infrastructure and careful client qualification. This model prices every project at 25-30% of the client's annual value saved, with no fixed tiers.

**Overall Weighted Score: 3.52/5**

This model excels at profitability and flexibility but struggles with simplicity and scalability, making it best suited for high-ticket implementations rather than volume-based growth.

---

## Evaluation Criteria Scores

### 1. Profitability (25% weight) - Score: 5/5

**Analysis:**
- Margins are maximized by anchoring to client value rather than cost
- A $500/month time savings becomes a $1,500-1,800/year fee instead of $500 flat
- Upfront payments (50%) improve cash flow dramatically
- No price ceiling - same work could be $5K or $50K depending on client

**Evidence:**
- Fitness Influencer saving 20hrs/month at $75/hr = $18,000/year value
- At 25-30%: $4,500-5,400 project fee
- Actual development time: ~40 hours at $100/hr = $4,000 cost
- Margin: $500-1,400 (11-26%)

**Profitability Calculation:**
| Client Size | Annual Value | Our Price (27.5%) | Est. Cost | Margin |
|-------------|--------------|-------------------|-----------|--------|
| Small (10hr/mo saved) | $9,000 | $2,475 | $2,000 | 19% |
| Medium (20hr/mo saved) | $18,000 | $4,950 | $4,000 | 19% |
| Large (40hr/mo saved) | $36,000 | $9,900 | $6,000 | 39% |
| Enterprise (80hr/mo saved) | $72,000 | $19,800 | $10,000 | 49% |

**Key Insight:** Margins improve dramatically with larger clients. This model naturally incentivizes pursuing bigger deals.

---

### 2. Simplicity (20% weight) - Score: 2/5

**Analysis:**
- Requires discovery call for EVERY prospect
- Client must understand and agree to ROI calculation
- Some clients resist sharing financial details
- "What's your price?" cannot be answered on website

**Friction Points:**
1. **No self-service:** Prospects can't buy without talking to you
2. **ROI calculation varies:** Time saved, revenue gained, cost avoided - which metric?
3. **Disagreements:** "I don't think we'd save that much"
4. **Trust barrier:** New clients skeptical of your ROI projections

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Create ROI calculator tool for self-assessment
- Publish case studies with specific value metrics
- Offer "value audit" as lead magnet
- Use conservative estimates (underpromise, overdeliver)

---

### 3. Scalability (20% weight) - Score: 2/5

**Analysis:**
- Each deal requires custom scoping
- Discovery calls take 30-60 minutes
- Proposal creation takes 1-2 hours per prospect
- Cannot delegate sales until process is systematized

**Bottleneck Analysis:**
| Activity | Time per Deal | Monthly Capacity (solo) |
|----------|---------------|------------------------|
| Lead qualification | 15 min | N/A |
| Discovery call | 45 min | 40 calls |
| ROI calculation | 30 min | 40 calculations |
| Proposal creation | 90 min | 26 proposals |
| Negotiation | 30 min | 40 negotiations |
| **Total sales time** | **3.5 hours** | **11 deals/month max** |

**At 30% close rate:** 11 proposals = 3-4 deals/month = ceiling without hiring

**Scaling Paths:**
1. **Productize discovery:** Create standardized questionnaire
2. **Hire sales:** Train someone on ROI calculation framework
3. **Hybrid model:** Fixed tiers for small deals, custom for large
4. **Qualify harder:** Only take calls with pre-qualified high-value prospects

---

### 4. Filtering (15% weight) - Score: 4/5

**Analysis:**
- Value-based pricing naturally repels price-shoppers
- Clients who engage with ROI discussion are serious
- Discovery process reveals red flags early
- High prices filter for clients who can afford you

**Client Quality Signals:**
| Signal | Good Fit | Poor Fit |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Discovery engagement | Eager to discuss ROI | "Just tell me the price" |
| Value articulation | Knows their costs | "I don't track that" |
| Decision process | Owner on call | "I need to ask my partner" |
| Budget discussion | Comfortable with value math | "That seems high" |

**Filtering Effectiveness:**
- Discovery call drops 50% of tire-kickers
- ROI discussion drops another 20%
- Final 30% are serious buyers
- Close rate on qualified calls: 40-60%

## Specific Questions Answered

### Q1: What's the minimum project value to make discovery calls worthwhile?

**Answer: $3,000 minimum project value**

**Math:**
- Discovery call + proposal = 3.5 hours
- At $150/hr effective rate target = $525 sales cost
- Need 15% margin minimum = $78
- Total cost to break even = $603
- At 30% close rate, need $2,009 average deal to cover sales costs
- Add 50% buffer = **$3,000 minimum**

**Implication:** Fitness influencer example ($4,950) works. A $1,500 project does not.

**Minimum Value Filter:**
- Time saved < 15 hours/month at $50/hr = Not worth discovery call
- Refer to productized solution or DIY resources

---

### Q2: How do you handle clients who can't articulate their ROI?

**Three Strategies:**

1. **Guide the calculation:**
   ```
   "How many hours per week do you spend on [task]?"
   "What's your time worth per hour?"
   "Over 12 months, that's $X in time alone"
   ```

2. **Use industry benchmarks:**
   - "Fitness influencers typically save 15-25 hours/month"
   - "HVAC quote automation reduces response time by 70%"
   - Position as "conservative estimate based on similar clients"

3. **Disqualify and redirect:**
   - "Sounds like you're earlier stage - here are some DIY resources"
   - "Let's reconnect when you have clearer metrics"
   - Don't chase clients who can't see value

**Red Flag:** If after 15 minutes they still can't articulate pain = wrong fit.

---

### Q3: What's the realistic close rate with this model?

**Answer: 25-35% on qualified leads**

**Funnel Breakdown:**
| Stage | Conversion | Notes |
|-------|------------|-------|
| Lead → Discovery call | 40% | Many won't book |
| Discovery → Proposal | 60% | Disqualify bad fits |
| Proposal → Close | 50% | Value-aligned buy |
| **Overall: Lead → Close** | **12%** | Industry average |

**For qualified leads only (past discovery):** 30-35% close rate

**Factors affecting close rate:**
- Quality of discovery (did you nail their pain?)
- ROI clarity (is the math undeniable?)
- Competition (are they shopping around?)
- Decision timeline (urgency matters)

### Q4: How does recurring revenue work (retainers)?

**Value-Based Retainer Model:**

1. **Initial Implementation:** One-time fee at 25-30% of Year 1 value
2. **Ongoing Maintenance:** Monthly retainer at 10-15% of monthly value captured

**Example:**
- Fitness Influencer saves $1,500/month
- Implementation: $4,500 (30% of $15,000 year 1 value)
- Monthly retainer: $150-225/month (10-15% of $1,500)
- Year 1 total: $4,500 + ($200 x 12) = $6,900
- Year 2+: $2,400/year passive

**Retainer Justification:**
- "Your automation saves you $1,500/month"
- "We ensure it keeps working perfectly"
- "If it breaks, we fix it within 24 hours"
- "$200/month is 13% insurance on your $1,500 monthly gain"

**Retention Rate Target:** 85%+ (if they're still getting value)

## Recommended Structure for Pure Value-Based

### Discovery Framework (30-45 min call)

**Phase 1: Pain Discovery (10 min)**
- "Walk me through your current workflow for [X]"
- "How many hours per week does this take?"
- "What would you do with that time back?"

**Phase 2: Value Quantification (15 min)**
- "What's your time worth per hour?"
- "Are there revenue opportunities you're missing?"
- "What's the cost of NOT solving this?"

**Phase 3: Solution Overview (10 min)**
- "Here's what we'd build..."
- "Timeline is typically X weeks"
- "We price at 25-30% of the value we create"

**Phase 4: Qualification (5 min)**
- "Does this budget range work for you?"
- "Who else needs to approve this?"
- "What's your timeline for making a decision?"

## Risk Assessment

### High Risks

1. **Client disputes ROI calculation**
   - Mitigation: Use conservative estimates, document assumptions
   - Severity: Medium (can lose deal)

2. **Can't scale without process**
   - Mitigation: Document discovery framework, create proposal templates
   - Severity: High (bottleneck at founder)

3. **Long sales cycles**
   - Mitigation: Qualify for urgency, create deadline incentives
   - Severity: Medium (cash flow impact)

### Medium Risks

4. **Market prefers fixed pricing**
   - Mitigation: Offer hybrid option for smaller deals
   - Severity: Medium (lose some segment)

5. **Difficulty training sales team**
   - Mitigation: Create playbook, record calls, shadow before solo
   - Severity: Medium (limits growth)

### Low Risks

6. **Competitor undercutting**
   - Mitigation: Differentiate on results, not price
   - Severity: Low (different buyer)

## Pros and Cons Summary

### Pros
1. **Highest possible margins** - No price ceiling
2. **Perfect client alignment** - Both parties win
3. **Natural qualification** - Discovery weeds out bad fits
4. **Premium positioning** - Attracts serious buyers
5. **Infinite flexibility** - Works for any project size

### Cons
1. **Time-intensive sales** - Every deal needs discovery
2. **Hard to scale** - Can't productize easily
3. **Requires confidence** - Must sell the value, not the work
4. **Some market resistance** - SMBs may want simpler buying
5. **Variable revenue** - Harder to predict monthly income

## Final Recommendation

**Pure Value-Based Pricing Score: 3.52/5**

**Best For:**
- High-ticket implementations ($5K+)
- Clients with clear, quantifiable pain
- Founder-led sales (for now)
- Building premium brand positioning

**Not Ideal For:**
- Volume-based growth
- Self-service buyers
- Small projects under $3K
- Scaling beyond founder quickly

**Recommended Hybrid Approach:**
1. Use pure value-based for deals $5K+
2. Create 2-3 fixed-price "starter" packages for smaller deals
3. Treat fixed-price as qualification for value-based relationship

2. Create ROI calculator tool for website
