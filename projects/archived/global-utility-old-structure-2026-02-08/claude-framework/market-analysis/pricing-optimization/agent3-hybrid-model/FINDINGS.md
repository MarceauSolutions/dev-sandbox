## Research Synthesis

### Why 3 Tiers is Optimal (Not 2)

**Research Evidence:**
- [Price Intelligently analyzed 512 SaaS companies](https://www.withorb.com/blog/tiered-pricing-examples) and found those with three tiers had **30% higher ARPU** than those with four or more tiers
- [ConversionXL study](https://www.saastock.com/blog/the-saas-pricing-trap-when-too-many-tiers-kill-conversions/) showed moving from four tiers to three increased conversion rates by **27%**
- [CXL Institute analysis of 280 SaaS pricing pages](https://fastspring.com/blog/three-tier-pricing-strategy-for-saas-is-it-still-ideal/) found **68% of successful SaaS companies use 3-4 pricing tiers**
- Conversion rates drop approximately **17%** when moving from 3 to 5+ options

**The Psychology:**
The "Good, Better, Best" structure aligns with how humans make decisions. [Columbia University research by Sheena Iyengar](https://www.getmonetizely.com/articles/customer-choice-overload-how-many-pricing-options-are-too-many) found that when shoppers were presented with 24 options, only 3% purchased - but with 6 options, purchase rates jumped to 30%.

**2-Tier Limitation:**
Two tiers creates a binary choice that feels limiting. Prospects think: "Am I a basic customer or a premium customer?" Three tiers provide a natural middle ground where most buyers cluster (the "decoy effect" makes the middle tier most attractive).

**Recommendation:** **3 setup tiers** for complexity differentiation, but **2 retainer tiers** (retainers are simpler - you either need basic support or strategic partnership).

## Critical Question Answers

### 1. Is 2 tiers or 3 tiers optimal for setup fees? Why?

**Answer: 3 tiers is optimal.**

Rationale:
- Automation projects naturally fall into three complexity buckets (single workflow vs. multi-workflow vs. AI-orchestrated)
- Research shows 30% higher ARPU with 3 tiers vs. 4+ tiers
- The middle tier captures the majority of buyers (decoy effect)
- 2 tiers forces a binary "basic/premium" choice that loses nuance

**Exception:** If more than 70% of deals naturally cluster into one tier, consider simplifying to 2.

### 2. Should community be entry point or separate product line?

**Answer: Separate product line, but leveraged as a lead funnel.**

Rationale:
- **Entry point model** creates expectation that $47/mo customers will upgrade to $3K+ services (conversion <3%)
- **Separate product model** positions community as its own value proposition with services as "professional tier"
- Community builds brand awareness and generates warm leads for discovery calls
- [Research shows freemium-to-paid conversion](https://userpilot.com/blog/saas-average-conversion-rate/) is typically 5-7% for sales-assisted models

**Optimal Structure:**
```
Community ($47/mo) → Discovers services exist → Discovery call → Setup + Retainer

OR

Direct lead → Discovery call → Setup + Retainer (Community included free for 6 months)
```

### 3. What's the minimum setup fee that filters price-shoppers?

**Answer: $2,997 minimum.**

Rationale:
- [B2B pricing research](https://www.pacepricing.com/blog/hidden-prices-lost-buyers-why-b2b-saas-companies-should-embrace-transparency) shows "a baseline price sets clear expectations and filters out prospects whose budgets aren't aligned"
- $2,500-3,000 is the "minimum viable serious buyer" threshold in automation
- Below $2K attracts DIY-ers who want to pay for templates
- Nick Saraev sells automation systems for $2K-$15K (this floor is validated)

**Supporting Evidence:**
- $2,997 = 3 days of focused work + template leverage
- At $150/hr effective rate (including sales time), $2,997 = 20 hours total
- Below this, the economics don't work for custom implementation

### 4. How do retainers relate to setup complexity?

**Answer: There's a natural correlation, but it's not 1:1.**

| Setup Tier | Expected Retainer | Conversion % |
|------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Starter ($2,997) | Maintenance ($747) | 60% retain |
| Growth ($7,497) | Maintenance or Partner | 70% retain |
| Enterprise ($14,997) | Partner ($2,247) | 85% retain |

Rationale:
- Simpler setups require less ongoing support (lower retainer)
- Complex AI systems require more maintenance (higher retainer)
- Enterprise clients expect strategic partnership (Partner tier default)
- [Digital Agency Network data](https://digitalagencynetwork.com/ai-agency-pricing/) shows retainer retention correlates with project complexity

**Key Insight:** Retainer upsell should happen AFTER setup proves value, not at initial close. "Let's get this running first, then discuss ongoing support in week 4."

### 5. What's the ideal LTV for a client (setup + 12mo retainer)?

**Answer: $12,000 - $42,000 per client.**

| Scenario | Setup | Retainer | 12mo LTV |

**Formula:**
```
LTV = Setup Fee + (Monthly Retainer × Average Retention Months)
```

[First Page Sage benchmark](https://firstpagesage.com/reports/average-customer-lifetime-value-ltv-clv-for-a-services-company-fc/) shows marketing agencies average $60,000 LTV with 24-month retention. Our model with 12-month assumption is conservative.

**Target LTV:CAC Ratio:** 3:1 or better
- If CAC = $500 (content + discovery call), LTV should be $1,500+ (easily achieved)
- If CAC = $2,000 (paid ads + 2 sales calls), LTV should be $6,000+ (achieved at Starter tier)
