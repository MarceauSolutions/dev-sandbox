# Nurture Email Optimization Strategy

> Guides the AutoIterator when proposing email nurture sequence variants.

## Domain Context

Marceau Solutions runs multi-touch email nurture sequences for B2B leads:
- **Target**: Local business owners (gyms, contractors, salons, restaurants) in Naples/Fort Myers, FL
- **Goal**: Convert cold leads into booked discovery calls
- **Sender**: William Marceau via wmarceau@marceausolutions.com (SMTP)
- **Current sequences**: website_builder (5-touch), automation (4-touch)

## Brand Voice (Email-Specific)

- **Subject lines**: Short (3-6 words), curiosity-driven, never clickbait
- **Tone**: Consultant, not salesperson. "I help businesses find where they're leaking time and money."
- **Opening**: Personal, specific. Never "Dear valued customer" or "I hope this finds you well."
- **Closing**: Soft CTA. "Would a quick call make sense?" not "SCHEDULE NOW"
- **Format**: Plain text preferred over heavy HTML (higher deliverability, more personal feel)
- **Length**: 3-5 sentences max. Brevity = respect for their time.

## Proven Patterns

1. **Question engagement**: Opening with a specific pain-point question gets 3x more replies
2. **Discovery framing**: "What's your biggest bottleneck?" outperforms solution-selling
3. **Breakup emails get highest response**: "Should I stop reaching out?" — 2-3x reply rate
4. **Social proof specificity**: "Helped 3 Naples gyms" > "helped many businesses"
5. **One CTA per email**: Multiple CTAs reduce click-through by 40%

## Compliance

- CAN-SPAM: Physical address + unsubscribe link required
- No misleading subject lines
- Identify as commercial message
- Honor opt-outs within 10 business days

## What to Optimize

- **Open rate** (0.3 weight): Subject line + preview text + send time
- **Reply rate** (0.4 weight): Body copy, question framing, CTA clarity
- **Unsubscribe rate** (-0.2 penalty): Relevance, frequency, value delivery
- **Conversion rate** (0.1 weight): Overall sequence effectiveness

## Variant Guidelines

- Optimize ONE element per variant (subject, opener, CTA, or body structure)
- Stay in consultant voice — never pivot to aggressive sales
- Keep emails under 100 words
- Test different pain points but keep the same warm, direct tone
