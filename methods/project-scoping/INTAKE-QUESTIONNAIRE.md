## Auto-Qualification Rules

### Proceed to Call If:

- Budget ≥ $1,500
- Timeline is realistic (not "ASAP" without good reason)
- Decision-maker identified
- Can articulate a problem/goal

### Flag for Caution If:

- Budget "Not sure yet" → Probe on call
- Multiple decision-makers → Establish single contact
- "ASAP" timeline → Understand why, quote rush fee
- Getting 3+ quotes → May be price shopping

### Consider Declining If:

- Budget under $1,500 → Refer to alternatives
- Can't articulate problem → "Just want it to look better"
- Vague on everything → Not ready for project

## Discovery Call Script (30-45 min)

### Opening (5 min)

```
"Thanks for taking the time, [Name]. I know you're busy running [business].

Before we dive in, I'd like to understand your business better so I can
see if I'm the right fit to help. Sound good?"

[RAPPORT] "How long have you been running [business name]?"
```

### Discovery (15-20 min)

**SITUATION** (Current state):
```
"Walk me through how customers typically find and book with you right now."
"What's working well?"
"What's frustrating?"
```

**PROBLEM** (Pain point):
```
"You mentioned [issue from form]. How is that affecting your business?"
"If you had to put a number on it, what's that costing you monthly?"
(lost bookings, time wasted, missed opportunities)
```

**IMPLICATION** (Stakes):
```
"If this stays the same for another year, what happens?"
"How does this compare to your competitors?"
```

**NEED-PAYOFF** (Desired outcome):
```
"If we could solve [problem], what would that mean for your business?"
"What would make this project a home run for you?"
```

### Qualification (10 min)

**BUDGET**:
```
"Projects like this typically range from $[X] to $[Y] depending on scope.
Does that align with what you had in mind?"
```

**TIMELINE**:
```
"When are you hoping to have this live?"
"Any specific events driving that date?"
```

**AUTHORITY**:
```
"Who else should be involved in this conversation?"
"What's your process for approving designs?"
```

**CONTENT**:
```
"Do you have your content ready - photos, service descriptions, pricing?"
"Who will be responsible for providing that?"
```

### Close (5 min)

**If Qualified**:
```
"Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm thinking... [brief solution].
This sounds like a [Tier] project in the $[X-Y] range.

Would you like me to put together a detailed proposal?"
```

**If Not Qualified**:
```
"I appreciate you sharing all this. To be honest, based on [budget/timeline/scope],
I'm not sure I'm the right fit for this project.

I'd recommend [Squarespace/Wix for budget, another provider for timeline, etc.]"
```

**Always**:
```
"What questions do you have for me?"

"Great. I'll send over a proposal by [day]. Can we schedule a 15-minute call
for [day] to review it together?"
```

## BANT+ Scoring Sheet

Complete during or immediately after discovery call.

### Score Each 1-5:

| Dimension | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | Score |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|---|-------|
| **Budget** | Won't discuss | Below minimum | Vague but open | In range | Clear match | ___ |
| **Authority** | Not involved | Needs approval | Partner involved | Key voice | Sole decider | ___ |
| **Need** | No clear need | Vague want | General goal | Clear problem | Quantified pain | ___ |
| **Timeline** | Impossible | Very tight | Tight but doable | Reasonable | Flexible | ___ |
| **Fit** | Red flags | Concerns | Neutral | Good signs | Great match | ___ |

**TOTAL**: ___/25

**Decision**:
- ≥20: Ideal client - prioritize
- 15-19: Good fit - proceed
- 10-14: Caution - consider declining or add protections
- <10: Decline or refer elsewhere

## Red Flags Checklist

### Tier 1: Immediate Disqualifiers

- [ ] "Can you match this $500 quote?"
- [ ] Requests spec work / free mockups
- [ ] "My last 3 designers were terrible"
- [ ] Won't sign contract
- [ ] No-shows or reschedules multiple times
- [ ] Disrespectful of your time

### Tier 2: Proceed with Caution

- [ ] "I'll know it when I see it"
- [ ] 4+ decision makers
- [ ] Won't discuss budget at all
- [ ] Hints at feature creep ("while you're at it...")
- [ ] Expects 24/7 availability

### Tier 3: Yellow Flags (Monitor)

- [ ] First-time website owner
- [ ] Very new business (<1 year)
- [ ] "My nephew started it but..."
- [ ] Wants to "build a platform"
- [ ] Compares to major tech products

## "I Don't Know What I Want" Handling

If client can't articulate needs, use these techniques:

### The Examples Approach
```
"No problem - let me ask it differently:
1. Show me 3 websites you love (any industry)
2. What do you like about each one?
3. Now show me one you hate - what bothers you?"
```

### The Goals Approach
```
"Let's forget the website for a second:
1. What's your #1 business goal this year?
2. What's the biggest friction your customers face?
3. If your website could only do ONE thing, what would it be?"
```

### The Competitor Approach
```
"Let's look at your competitors:
1. Who do you lose business to most often?
2. What do they do better than you online?
3. What do YOU do better that doesn't show on your current site?"
```

### The Day-in-the-Life Approach
```
"Walk me through what happens when someone finds your business:
1. How do they typically find you?
2. What questions do they ask before booking?
3. What makes someone choose you over competitors?"
```
